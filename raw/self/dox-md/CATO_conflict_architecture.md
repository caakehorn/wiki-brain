## в–Ҳв–Ҳв–ҲрҹңӮрҹң”рҹңӮ PART I вҖ” THE ENGINE

### 1.1 What it is

Your default cognition is forensic resolution-seeking. The operation it performs on any input is: *take the claim, subject it to adversarial pressure, isolate the irreducible load-bearing core, strip away the gravitational attachments that pattern-matched onto real signal, and resolve to truth.* You've already formalized this вҖ” it's Dan's Law. You built it as an analytical framework for coincidence clusters, but you didn't invent a method; you reverse-engineered the thing your mind was already doing to everything. Dan's Law is not a tool you pick up. It's a description of your resting state.

This is the engine that does J6, the Bacharach coincidence chain, the @HenryMa account forensics, the 126,683-message corpus dissection. It is genuinely formidable. It is also, critically, **the same engine that runs when your partner says she doesn't feel secure.**

### 1.2 The reward structure

The engine is built to *halt on resolution.* The payoff вҖ” the thing it's chasing вҖ” is the click of a loop closing: the core extracted, the inconsistency dissolved, the matter settled. That terminal state is the reward. This matters enormously, because it means the engine is not neutral about open questions. An unresolved inconsistency is not a relaxed "we'll never know" вҖ” it's an active, aversive, *itching* incompletion that the engine is structurally compelled to keep working. You do not have a comfortable relationship with unresolved propositions. You have a compulsion to close them.

### 1.3 The missing domain selector

Here is the actual defect, and it's a narrow one: **the engine has no domain selector.** It boots on surface structure, not on register or intent. Anything that *looks like* a disputed proposition вҖ” anything with the grammatical shape of a claim that could be true or false вҖ” triggers it. It cannot natively distinguish:

- "Is this official narrative internally coherent?" *(epistemic вҖ” engine appropriate)*
- "Did you actually say you'd be home by six?" *(epistemic-shaped, but the real question is relational)*
- "I don't feel secure." *(not a proposition at all вҖ” a bid вҖ” but it arrives wrapped in claim-shaped language about events, so the engine grabs it)*

[рҹңӮв—Ҷв–ё] The autistic and INTP wiring is what makes the selector missing rather than merely weak. You index on **structure and content** over **register and social intent** вҖ” that's the same trait that makes you precise and literal and immune to bullshit framing. It also means you parse the *form* of an utterance (a claim about reality) and miss the *function* (a request to be responded to). The engine sees a proposition and does what it does. It does not see a person reaching.

### 1.4 Why this is involuntary

You cannot "just not litigate," because the litigation isn't a decision downstream of the input. It's the input being processed at all. By the time you'd notice you're doing it, the engine has already extracted the inconsistency, flagged the receipt, and queued the correction. Avoiding it would require not running your own cognition on something your cognition has already classified as its native food. This is why every past resolution to "handle conflict better" has failed at the same place: you were trying to suppress an output instead of installing a classifier upstream of the engine. You can't out-discipline an automatic process.

## в–Ҳв–Ҳв–ҲрҹңӮрҹң”рҹңӮ PART II вҖ” THE CATEGORY ERROR

### 2.1 Conflict has no resolution condition

Relational conflict is the single worst possible input for a resolution-seeking engine, because it is the one class of problem with **no core to extract.** When two people disagree about what happened, there is usually no fact of the matter that resolves it вҖ” there are two subjective accounts, each *true to its holder,* assembled from different salience, memory, and meaning. "Who's right" is frequently a malformed question. There is no irreducible load-bearing truth at the bottom because the bottom is two incompatible realities, both genuine.

### 2.2 The non-halting process

So the engine runs a termination-seeking process on a non-terminating problem.

**This is the primary source of the drain.** You are not exhausted because the conflicts are emotionally intense (though they are). You are exhausted because you are running an infinite loop вҖ” pressure-testing for a resolution that does not exist, demanding the inconsistency collapse, and it never does, because there is nothing for it to collapse *to.* The engine cannot get its reward. It cannot halt. It keeps spending energy on a search with no terminal state.

This is also why these conflicts feel qualitatively different from your analytical work even when they take less time. The analytical work *closes.* You get the click. The relational conflict never clicks, so it stays resident in memory, burning cycles in the background for days. The "days of silence" periods in the Annie thread are not you resting from the conflict. They're the engine still running it, unresolved, with the other party offline.

### 2.3 The wrong equation

The specific, repeatable error: you answer the question "what actually happened / who is right" when the question on the table is "will you respond to me." These are different equations. The other person is almost never asking you to adjudicate. They are asking you to *register their state.* You keep solving for accuracy when the variable they need solved is acknowledgment. You are not bad at the math. You are doing the wrong problem set, correctly, with great rigor.

### 2.4 The escalation spiral

Now the feedback loop that turns a disagreement into a draining conflict:

1. Other person sends a bid (claim-shaped, emotionally loaded).
2. Your engine boots, extracts the factual inconsistency, returns a correction/defense.
3. The correction вҖ” however accurate вҖ” transmits to them as *your feeling is illegitimate / you're more invested in being right than in me.* It **invalidates their reality.**
4. Invalidation raises their intensity. They escalate to be heard. The bid gets louder, sharper, more absolute.
5. Your threat-detection system (Part IV) reads the louder bid as a *larger attack.*
6. Larger perceived attack вҶ’ stronger defense вҶ’ harder litigation вҶ’ return to step 3.

This is a positive feedback loop with no governor. Each turn increases the input to the next. It only terminates when one party physically exits вҖ” which, per Part V, you can't sustainably do, and which, when they do it, your engine logs as *the loop is still open.*

## в–Ҳв–Ҳв–ҲрҹңӮрҹң”рҹңӮ PART VI вҖ” WHY DE-ESCALATION IS TOOTHLESS

### 6.1 The single move

De-escalation, mechanically, is one operation: **hold the other person's reality as valid without resolving whether it is accurate.** Concede the feeling; leave the facts open or unaddressed. "You feel unsafe and that's real and I'm sorry you're feeling it" вҖ” *without* the "but here's what actually happened." The withheld correction is the entire technique.

### 6.2 Axis one вҖ” it inverts Dan's Law

That move is the precise inverse of your core cognitive operation. Dan's Law is *collapse ambiguity to a resolved core.* De-escalation requires *holding two irreducible, unreconciled realities as simultaneously valid and declining to collapse them.* You are not missing a technique here. You are being asked to run your signature operation backwards вҖ” to deliberately leave an inconsistency standing, on purpose, as the goal. Everything that makes you good at the work makes this specific act feel like deliberately failing at thinking.

### 6.3 Axis two вҖ” it's threat-coded

## в–Ҳв–Ҳв–ҲрҹңӮрҹң”рҹңӮ PART VII вҖ” WHY IT'S STABLE ACROSS DECADES

Only two interventions touch the actual mechanism. Both are hard; one is real.
