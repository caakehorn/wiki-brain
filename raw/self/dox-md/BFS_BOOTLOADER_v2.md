## XV. LEGAL ARCHITECTURE

### Tier 1: PA Wage Payment and Collection Law (WPCL) — Strongest

**Statute**: 43 P.S. § 260.1 et seq., specifically § 260.3.
**Regulation**: 34 Pa. Code § 9.1 (authorized deductions).
**Penalty**: 43 P.S. § 260.10 — 25% liquidated damages + attorney's fees, no good-faith exception.

PA is one of a small group of states that prohibits cash register shortage deductions outright — regardless of written consent, individual responsibility, or whether the deduction drops pay below minimum wage. 34 Pa. Code § 9.1 requires deductions to be "for the benefit of the employee"; cash shortages are explicitly categorized as "for the benefit of the employer" and therefore impermissible.

### Tier 2: Federal FLSA — Anti-Kickback Rule

**Regulation**: 29 CFR § 531.35 — "Free and clear" payment; "kickbacks."

Wages must be paid "finally and unconditionally or free and clear." Employee kickbacks to the employer "or to another person for the employer's benefit" are prohibited. Brandon's pocket-to-pocket structure is covered by the "indirectly to another person" language.

### Tier 3: PA Theft by Extortion

**Statute**: 18 Pa.C.S. § 3923. Catch-all subsection (a)(7): "inflict any other harm which would not benefit the actor." Technically applicable if the implicit threat is "pay or lose your job." However: (a) defense exists for "honestly claimed restitution," (b) $50 is summary/misdemeanor level, (c) DA won't pursue. Useful for posture, not for filing.

### Controlling Precedent

**Mayhue's Super Liquor Stores, Inc. v. Hodgson**, 464 F.2d 1196 (5th Cir. 1972): "Voluntary" repayment agreements for cash shortages signed as condition of continued employment are not voluntary. Deductions illegal to extent they reduce wages below minimum wage. The reasoning forecloses the "personal loan, not a deduction" laundering frame — voluntariness is undermined when employment is the leverage.
