# GPS ANALYSIS

A behavioral/psychographic forensic analysis of Google Location History
data, distinct from the raw visit-count catalog on
wiki/self/location-history.md. Two report variants exist: a general
"Core Metrics" pass and a more detailed 2014-2020 aggregate pass with
named life-phase chapters.

## Core Metrics (general pass)

- Home Anchoring Index: 0.68 (moderate-high) — ~68% of time within a
  tight radius of primary residence.
- Exploration Ratio (novelty vs routine): 0.22 (low).
- Mean Radius of Gyration: 15.8 km.
- Routine Index (predictability): 0.85 (very high).
- Most active hours: 2-6 PM. Least active: 3-7 AM.
- Most active day: Friday ("release valve" signature — spike in travel
  distance and location variety). Least active: Sunday.
- Location Type Diversity Index: 0.35 (low-moderate).
- Activity Diversity Index: 0.28 (low) — dominant logged activities are
  "in vehicle," "at home," "at work."

## Life Phase Markers (general pass)

- Phase 1: The Pennsylvanian Period (2014-2019) — Uniontown center,
  smallest radius, most predictable.
- Phase 2: The Metropolitan Experiment (2019-2024) — NYC center,
  expanded radius, core routine patterns held despite the more complex
  environment.
- Anomaly 1 (2014): brief cluster in a novel location — possibly the
  "5-day smol bean magic pixie dream girl" incident referenced
  elsewhere.

## 2014-2020 Aggregate Pass — Named Phases

- **The Forge (2014):** baseline extreme stability, Uniontown, small
  predictable loop.
- **The Binary System (2015-2017):** a primary relationship (Annie)
  introduces a two-node oscillation between Saratoga Dr. and Virginia
  Ave; increasing strain ("The Great Unsettling" of 2017) as the
  two-node system's energy cost becomes unsustainable.
- **System Collapse & Migration (2018-2019):** the binary system fails
  catastrophically in 2018 (spatial chaos, high mileage — over a third
  of the whole 2014-2020 aggregate distance traveled happens in this
  one year alone), culminating in the 2019 move to NYC and the new
  anchor at 307 E 76th St.
- **The Siege of Manhattan (2020):** COVID forces compression into a
  micro-radius around the new home anchor.

Aggregate stats for 2014-2020: total distance ~170,000 km (over 4x
Earth's circumference), ~3,500 unique locations visited. Most-visited
non-home location, Uniontown era: Walmart Supercenter. Most-visited
non-home location, NYC era: Central Park (described as "primary release
valve and psychological decompression zone" during 2020 lockdown). In
NYC specifically, "The Stand" comedy club is named as a recurring
Friday-night social-release location.

## Psychographic Inferences (speculative, AI-generated)

Paradoxical stability/volatility: a meticulous architect of stable,
predictable systems who nonetheless demonstrates system-shattering
volatility when a chosen framework stops working — read as "systemic
integrity" as the core drive rather than stability itself. Novelty-
seeking read as low-baseline but high-as-tactical-tool (deployed hard
during life-phase transitions, not day to day). Risk/impulsivity read
as low in daily movement but capable of huge, decisive life-altering
gambles — offered as a reconciliation with the self-reported high
day-to-day impulsivity trait documented elsewhere (this trait manifests
in social/intellectual domains, not physical navigation). Sociality
read as scheduled and contained — social energy released in structured,
predictable weekly bursts (Friday) rather than integrated fluidly
through the week.
