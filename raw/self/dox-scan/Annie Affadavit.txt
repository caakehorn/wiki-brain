AFFIDAVIT of PROBABLE CAUSE

Your affiant is Trooper Jeremy MILLER of the Pennsylvania State Police, Troop B, Uniontown patrol section.

On 10/26/25, I was on patrol operating marked patrol unit B4-14, working the 2200–0600-hour shift, partnered with Trooper Dylan R. WHITE. At approximately 0049 hours, I was traveling west on Route 40, Uniontown City, Fayette County, PA. I traversed the intersection of US Route 40 and proceeded to turn left onto S Mount Vernon Ave, Uniontown City, Fayette County, PA. As I was traversing the intersection, I observed a black sedan stationary at the intersection facing my patrol vehicle. I observed this vehicle to have its high beams active and straddling the lane designators between the straight and right turn lanes. After observing this, I proceeded to drive past the vehicle and turn my patrol vehicle around, so that I was positioned behind the vehicle. At this point, I observed the PA Registration to be MWT6743. I proceeded to following this vehicle through the intersection and travel north on N Mount Vernon Ave. The operator then activated a left turn signal to turn into the parking lot of Walgreens, which is a closed business. At this point, I active my emergency lights and siren and a traffic stop was conducted in the 1st block of N Mount Vernon Ave, Uniontown City, Fayette County, PA in the parking lot of Walgreens.
I approached the passenger side of the vehicle, and my partner Trooper WHITE approached the driver side. Trooper WHITE initially made contact with the operator, namely Anne ULMER, who provided him with her PA Driver’s License. While speaking with Trooper WHITE, I could detect a strong odor of an alcoholic beverage emanating from within the vehicle while on the passenger side. At this point, Trooper WHITE proceeded to return to our patrol vehicle to conduct a query of ULMER and I made contact with ULMER at the driver window. I requested ULMER provide me with her registration and insurance. ULMER was unable to provide the correct requested documents. During this interaction with ULMER, I immediately detected a strong odor of an alcoholic beverage emanating from her person. I also observed ULMER to appear dazed and have watery eyes. I asked ULMER how much she had to drink. ULMER related “I have not drank that much”.

At this point, I requested ULMER exit the vehicle. I then requested ULMER walk back to my patrol vehicle, to which she complied. Once back at my patrol vehicle, I requested ULMER participate in Standardized Field Sobriety Tests (SFSTs). The only SFST conducted was the Horizontal Gaze Nystagmus test (HGN), due to ULMER informing me she had a broken ankle. After the conclusion of SFSTs, I asked ULMER what she had drank. She related “Tito and Soda”. In addition to SFSTs, I requested ULMER blow into a Preliminary Breath Test (PBT). ULMER was unable to follow instructions for blowing into the PBT.

After the conclusion of my roadside assessment, ULMER was taken into custody, without incident, and placed in the back of my patrol unit.

At approximately 0104 hours, ULMER was read the DL26-B form verbatim. ULMER refused to submit to a chemical test.

Based on the facts set forth within this Affidavit of Probable Cause, I respectfully request the Defendant answer to the charges listed on this Criminal Complaint.
