# Emotional Imprinting

Both dossier sources make the same claim in nearly the same language:
"unemotional sex is a myth" for Dan. Every high-investment sexual or
romantic encounter is described as producing immediate attachment
activation — `Dan Profile.txt` calls it "crush activation on contact" —
rather than staying casual or contained. The report frames this as the
direct mechanism behind two otherwise-separate facts already documented
elsewhere in the wiki: the intensity of Dan's relationship attachments,
and the disproportionate severity of the [[wiki/people/eli|Eli]] betrayal's
psychological impact. In this framing, the Eli incident wasn't processed
as ordinary romantic disappointment — it's described as a "temple
desecration," the violation of something categorically sacred rather than
merely painful.

## The mechanism as described

The claim has two parts. First, that encounters — not just relationships,
but individual high-investment sexual encounters — trigger what the
report calls "emotional imprinting": the other person is "absorbed and
mapped into his internal architecture" essentially on contact, not
gradually. Second, that this absorption is what makes betrayal or
disappointment catastrophic rather than merely disappointing: "contamination
occurs when reality fails to align with this idealized map," producing
what the same report elsewhere calls the shift from a partner as "chaotic
mirror" to "dissociated saboteur" — the exact language independently used
to describe the Eli incident's effect on how Dan modeled
[[wiki/people/annie-ulmer|Annie]] afterward (see
[[wiki/timeline/events/eli-incident]]).

## Where this converges with independently-documented material

Unlike the taboo-and-boundary-testing claim, this mechanism has real
convergent support, even though it originates in the same single-source
dossier. [[wiki/mind/concepts/attachment-model]] — built from a much
broader evidence base, including the primary message corpus rather than a
single AI report — independently documents an attachment-activation
asymmetry: nothing before Annie is recorded as triggering full attachment
activation, and once triggered, no natural mechanism existed in Dan's own
behavior to close the loop without an explicit severance signal from the
other person. That is a structurally identical claim to "crush activation
on contact" with no off switch, arrived at from different source material.
The Fe-starvation profile on [[wiki/mind/profile/intp]] (Fe 10% valuing,
46% active — the weak-but-craved feeling function) provides a plausible mechanism for why
this would be true: a system starved of the feeling function it needs
would be expected to over-attach rapidly to whatever supplies it, rather
than titrate exposure gradually the way a securely-resourced Fe user
might.

## What this does not explain

The emotional-imprinting claim, taken at face value, sits in some tension
with the orchestration/voyeurism pattern documented on
[[wiki/mind/psychosexual/orchestration-and-voyeurism]]: if every
high-investment encounter triggers total imprinting, the mechanism doesn't
obviously predict why Dan would then structure years of his primary
relationship around watching his partner have exactly those
high-investment encounters with other people, rather than avoiding the
exposure. The dossier doesn't resolve this tension explicitly — the
closest it comes is the claim that orchestration lets Dan extract meaning
from the *fact* of the encounter (authorship over the boundary) rather
than from direct sensory/emotional participation, which would in theory
route around the imprinting mechanism rather than triggering it. This is
plausible but unconfirmed by any primary source; it should be read as an
open structural question in the framework, not a resolved one.

**Gaps:** as with the rest of this cluster, the imprinting claim itself is
single-sourced to AI-authored analysis. Its convergence with
[[wiki/mind/concepts/attachment-model]] is real and independently
supported, but that convergence is at the level of *pattern* (rapid,
irreversible attachment activation), not at the level of the specific
"crush activation on contact" or "temple desecration" language, which
remains interpretive framing rather than primary-source fact.
