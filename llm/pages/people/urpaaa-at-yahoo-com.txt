# Contact urpaaa@yahoo.com

The owner of `urpaaa@yahoo.com` is one of the corpus's genuinely
unresolved close relationships: a **teacher** in a **parental register**
who, in a two-week window of autumn 2017 (23 received messages,
September 28 – October 11, plus Dan's sent side preserved in the
complete dump), gave Dan medical advice for an infected tooth, offered
unprompted to come along to the extraction — "Do u want me to go w u
tomorrow?" — drove out on the morning of October 6 ("Leaving in a
minute / Omw"), and was thanked in terms Dan reserves for almost no one:
"thanks a bunch for helping me get that done. and for coming. i really
appreciate it." Whoever this is, they mattered; the corpus just never
says their name.

## The evidence profile

The thread supports a specific composite. They **taught school** —
"I'm teaching now," "I'm in class," three times across mid-day windows.
They were **older and long-local**: when Dan passed along a greeting
from "chuck from tri county" (Tri County Tire, after a tire pop), the
reply was first-name obituary knowledge — "Maybe Chris? Definitely not
Chuck. He passed away last year." They followed **Laurel Highlands
football** with a partisan's reflexes: told of talk about Uniontown
losing, they shot back "It's not like Uniontown just started to suck…
Talk should be how LH beat WM." Their texting style runs on the
abbreviation set ("u," "ur," "w," "Do u want me to go w u") that the
corpus otherwise associates with the older Uniontown generation. And
they held a **standing care role**: the antibiotics triage
(MedExpress vs. the dentist, "dr.harr"), the post-operative follow-ups
("How do u feel?" / "How's ur mouth feeling?" / "rinse w salt water
every hour"), and a check-in four days later that closes the thread —
"Feeling any better."

Dan's side reciprocates the intimacy. He narrates his workday to them
unprompted (report times, the $100 Venmo tip — "That's cool. $100 tip.
Not bad," they answer), works a 5.5-hour loop the day after the
extraction because "i would suffer a brutal ball-busting if i took a
day off because a tooth hurt lol," and — most tellingly — reports the
evening crisis of October 7 as to family: "no relaxing. gram is
bleeding. hit her arm on the chair. i'm only one here." Casual
caregiving bulletins about [[wiki/people/fran-coldren|Fran]] went only
to the innermost circle.

Two later fragments confirm the handle's owner rather than identify
them: Dan, on getting a new phone (October 13, 2017), texts that he
"still hadn't updated your contact with your urpaaa email"; and in
March 2018 the contact writes "I found the receipt for my Macbook in my
urpaaa account. May 11 2013," followed by "Oooops misfire."

## Who it isn't, and who it might be

[[wiki/people/suzanne-frank|Suz]] and [[wiki/people/annie-ulmer|Annie]]
are excluded — both were receiving the same September 28 tooth
bulletins on their own known handles in parallel. The abbreviation
style, the auto-trade social fluency (knowing Tri County Tire's staff
by name is natural for the owner of an auto-parts business), and the
parental register are all consistent with
[[wiki/people/rick-frank|Rick Frank]] — but Rick's documented
occupation is retail entrepreneur, and nothing in his record has him
teaching classes, so the identification cannot be asserted. A partner
or relative on the paternal side (Rick's "Lisa," who has no known
handle) fits the care role equally well. Per the corpus's own
discipline: do not speculate past the evidence.
