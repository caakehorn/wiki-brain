# Nick Mattie

Nick Mattie was an eleven-week fixture of Dan's spring 2017
(`+17242526986`, 170 messages, April 20 – July 12, 2017) — a Uniontown
drug-scene peer with whom Dan ran a genuinely **reciprocal** trading
relationship: each man sourced for, fronted to, and borrowed from the
other, with the running balance tracked in Nick's hand-kept ledger.
"25 from the start plus 50 I gave you tonight. Minus 15 you gave me,"
he itemizes on May 11 — "Yeah man I try to keep a pretty decent book."
The thread burns extraordinarily hot (some 160 of its 170 messages fall
in the three weeks between April 29 and May 12) and then dies to three
unanswered fragments over two months, the last of them — "The hell are
you doing duder" — closing the record on July 12. It is the corpus's
cleanest specimen of the peer-trading tier of the
[[wiki/mind/synthesis/supply-network|supply network]]: below the
professional vendors, below even the middlemen, a layer where supply
was social reciprocity between two broke men.

The record is his side only (all rows marked Received). His register is
its own texture — "brotha man," "duder," "Ma fuckin dude" — warm,
chatty, and constitutionally unable to hold a grudge about money.

## The reciprocal machine

Neither man was consistently the dealer. In the opening exchange Nick
asks *Dan* where "to grab anything with the quickness" before work; ten
days later he's offering Dan "one 50 left until I get home from work";
by early May he's asking Dan for "that other 40" while simultaneously
running to his own upstream source for Dan — "He wants me to cop alone.
But it only takes me ten minutes from my place. You can just wait at my
place til I get back." Cash moved in both directions in the same
denominations ($20s, $40s, $50s, product unnamed throughout), cigarettes
rode along ("Good on the squares"), and weed was the social layer rather
than the commodity — "I'll run in and blow one down with you" while a
friend named Paul waited in the car. The fronts were bilateral and so
was the forbearance: "I can't really put much out on a limb this time
but you know I'll work with you."

The thread also quietly extends a structural finding: on May 2, 2017,
with Dan carrying "a buck" ($100) to work as debt repayment and no time
to double back, the handoff ran through
[[wiki/people/annie-ulmer|Annie]] — "She just messaged me on FB. Cool
to leave it with her? … It's there." The supply-network page documents
Annie as logistics facilitator in the 2018–19 Johnny era; the Nick
ledger shows her in that role a full year earlier.

## Texture of a Uniontown spring

The non-commerce residue is small-town life whole: Nick's place at "the
old Howell house"; runs past Tobacco World; a nephew who stayed over
and "got me for over nine bucks worth of candy"; Andy's baby shower,
which Nick both paid toward and used as his delivery window ("I can
just come to your place on my way there"); a paycheck that came up
wrong ("What happened with your check? … Talk about unfortunate"); a
new job started May 8 ("I can't be up too too late tonight. I start
that new gig in the morning"); and one glimpse of Dan's own year —
"And did you get your drums?" (May 6), a stray marker of gear
acquisition in the middle of the poverty floor. The thread's comic
peak is timestamped 3:55 AM: "How about chick tried adding me on
Facebook."

The ending needs no drama to explain it. Dan's side of the frequency
simply stops after May 12; Nick's last three messages over eight weeks
("Wanna do me favor for something in return?", "Can you locate", "The
hell are you doing duder") go nowhere. Given the ledger's mutual-debt
mechanics, an unresolved balance is a plausible but undocumented cause;
equally plausible is the ordinary churn of the
[[wiki/timeline/periods/2017-poverty-floor|2017 floor]], where most
relationships in the corpus run hot and vanish.
