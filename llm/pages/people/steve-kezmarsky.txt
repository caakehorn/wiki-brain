# Steve Kezmarsky

Steve Kezmarsky is a high-school-era Uniontown friend whose 66-message
thread (handle `+17244157286`, January 2017 – January 2018) documents
golf enthusiasm, unusually explicit self-diagnosis, and a life that
collapsed at the end of the thread — not through his own death, but
through his father's. As of December 2018 Dan describes him to
[[wiki/people/new-jim-shaffer|Jim Shaffer]] as alive, sober, a father of
two, and "living the least enviable life EVER": his father had been
arrested in January 2018 and was, in Dan's words, "going to jail for the
rest of his life," leaving the "family in ruins." He said he had two
young children ("a boy and a girl to carry out my legacy").

> **REVISED [2026-07-19]:** This page previously stated Steve was dead
> by December 2018, citing the Jim Shaffer thread. The raw rows show the
> opposite: Jim's "Wait... he's dead?" was a misreading of Dan's joke,
> and Dan immediately corrects him — "No no I mean / He's alive but
> living the least enviable life EVER / two kids / Sober / Dad going to
> jail for the rest of his life / Family in ruins" (2018-12-09, Jim
> Shaffer thread, `all_imessages_complete_dump.txt`). The death claim is
> withdrawn.

## The father's fall

The corpus tracks the elder Kezmarsky's collapse across three dated
points, none of which name the offense. On January 22, 2018 Annie
reports "They picked up mr Kezmarsky today" — an arrest. On April 22,
2018 Dan asks Jim "did you hear about mr.kezmarsky / he made quite the
legal oopsie goof," and Jim answers "Yes. He's fucked." By December 9,
2018 the sentence expectation is life imprisonment. Dan's own feeling
toward the man is unambiguous — "he deserves a malignant tumor too," "i
wish he got smashed by a drunk driver" — hostility whose origin the
corpus does not explain.

The arrest also resolves the timing of Steve's final messages: his last
run (January 30–31, 2018) — "It's been a rough week… my life is in
shambles," and "it took this happening for a dr to finally
pr[escribe]…" — comes eight days after his father was picked up. "This
happening" reads as the arrest, and the prescription as the medical
system finally responding to a man whose family had just detonated. The
thread simply stops there; everything after it comes secondhand.

## The friendship on the record

The thread's texture (his side only — the export's direction field marks
every row Received, and here that appears literally true of the
surviving rows) runs on two registers. The first is golf as friendship
currency: selling his TaylorMade M2 driver, buying the new one, his
irons and Scotty Cameron putter stolen from his garage (May 2017),
asking Dan to price him last year's TaylorMade PSis through a
connection, "Dude I wanna play golf so bad." In July 2017, mid-caddying
era for Dan, he asked to be gotten in at the club — then admitted weeks
later he'd never submitted the resume.

The second register is unusually explicit self-knowledge. In a single
January 2017 exchange he calls himself "a very sick person… A dark and
tormented soul to say the least," predicts that any woman around him
long enough would end up "just as fucked up as the rest of us… a drug
induced comatose state for 5-10 years," and lands the thread's most
striking line about the friendship itself: **"I've always respected the
fact that our friendship is based on my sickness."** A July 2017 message
asks after "vikes or anything small" — placing him on the consumer edge
of the same Uniontown informal-pharmacy world documented in
[[wiki/mind/synthesis/supply-network]], though he is not a supply node.
The sobriety Dan reports in December 2018 — framed to Jim with mock
cruelty as part of the punishment ("Let him live...but he'll be sober") —
is the thread's off-screen epilogue: the self-described sick man got
clean in the worst year of his life.

There is one further corpus appearance worth recording: in May 2016 Dan
tells Annie that Alexis once "accidentally sent me pictures of her
fucking Steve Kezmarsky" — and in his last message to Steve (January 31,
2018) Dan jokes about "the classic Steve Kezmarsky opportunity move of
fucking/stealing my girl." The friendship carried a documented history
of Steve crossing that line, held in the register of a running joke.
