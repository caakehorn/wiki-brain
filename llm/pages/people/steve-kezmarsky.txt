# Steve Kezmarsky

Steve Kezmarsky was a high-school-era Uniontown friend whose 66-message
thread (handle `+17244157286`, January 2017 – January 2018) reads, in
hindsight, like a compressed elegy: golf enthusiasm, open self-diagnosis,
a worsening rough patch — and then, within months of the last message,
his death. By December 2018 the news had reached Dan's circle:
[[wiki/people/new-jim-shaffer|Jim Shaffer]], another shared contact from
the same era, learned of it in his own thread with Dan and was stunned.
The cause and exact date are not in the corpus. He said he had two young
children ("a boy and a girl to carry out my legacy").

The thread's texture (his side only — the export's direction field marks
every row Received, and here that appears literally true of the surviving
rows) runs on two registers. The first is golf as friendship currency:
selling his TaylorMade M2 driver, buying the new one, his irons and
Scotty Cameron putter stolen from his garage (May 2017), asking Dan to
price him last year's TaylorMade PSis through a connection, "Dude I wanna
play golf so bad." In July 2017, mid-caddying-era for Dan, he asked to be
gotten in at the club — then admitted weeks later he'd never submitted
the resume.

The second register is unusually explicit self-knowledge. In a single
January 2017 exchange he calls himself "a very sick person… A dark and
tormented soul to say the least," predicts that any woman around him
long enough would end up "just as fucked up as the rest of us… a drug
induced comatose state for 5-10 years," and lands the thread's most
striking line about the friendship itself: **"I've always respected the
fact that our friendship is based on my sickness."** A July 2017 message
asks after "vikes or anything small" — placing him on the consumer edge
of the same Uniontown informal-pharmacy world documented in
[[wiki/mind/synthesis/supply-network]], though he is not a supply node.

The final run (January 30–31, 2018) opens with him not recognizing Dan's
number ("Sorry I lost a bunch of numbers") and closes the record on a
low: "It's been a rough week… my life is in shambles," and a line that
reads differently knowing what followed — "it took this happening for a
dr to finally pr[escribe]…" The thread simply stops there.
