# Josh Brannan

Josh Brannan is a Uniontown-era friend Dan reconnects with directly
once in the preserved corpus — a single warm, joking Facebook exchange
on February 27, 2017 — and who otherwise persists as a recurring
reference point in Dan's friendship with
[[wiki/people/jerad-friedline|Jerad Friedline]], where a private joke
about Josh eventually gets crystallized into an AI-generated audio file
years later.

## The 2017 reconnection

The thread opens with Dan initiating after apparent distance — "I miss
the fuck out of u. Call me when u get the chance sometime" — followed
immediately by his phone number, and quickly turns into rapid-fire
old-friend banter: jokes about mutual friends Shane and Zach, a beat of
real sadness when Josh mentions his grandmother's stroke ("Fuuuuuuuuck
dude that's awful I LOVED your grandma"), and nostalgia for ski trips
neither has taken in years ("it's way past due that we have a reunion.
Rick sold the condo so that's a bummer" — a reference to Dan's father,
[[wiki/people/rick-frank|Rick]]). The exchange turns candid when Dan
volunteers, unprompted, the nature of his then-current work: "My job is
fucking my girlfriend on webcam... Slash golf caddy," followed by "DUDE
IM A MODEL WE ARE MODEL BROS... Omg like $200 for an hour show" — an
open, joking acknowledgment of the camming work documented elsewhere in
the corpus, delivered to an old friend with no defensiveness. Josh's
reaction is unbothered amusement ("Dan frank's sex dungeon... Damn
making bank").

## The running joke

Separately, per `operating_manual.md`, Josh functions as a piece of
shared Uniontown mythology in Dan's ongoing friendship with Jerad — "a
Latrobe football legend" remembered for driving an Acura Integra
recklessly. In May 2025, Dan used a new AI text-to-speech tool to
synthesize a childhood memory involving Josh into an audio file titled
"josh brannan is innocent.wav," which operating_manual.md records as
one of the last messages in the entire Jerad thread at that point — the
specific eighth-grade memory behind the joke, and its place in the
broader pattern of Dan using Uniontown callbacks as a bonding ritual, is
documented on [[wiki/people/jerad-friedline]].
