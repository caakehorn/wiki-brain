# 117 Belmont Circle

117 Belmont Circle, Uniontown PA — a three-story house sitting on the edge
of the Uniontown Country Club fairway — was
[[wiki/people/fran-coldren|Fran Whyel Coldren's]] home for more than fifty
years, the command center of the Whyel/Frank family's country-club era, and
the site of Dan and [[wiki/people/annie-ulmer|Annie]]'s paid 24/7 caregiving years (~2015–2018). It is
also, structurally, where the Dan/Annie relationship was fated: **Annie
Ulmer's grandparents lived in the directly adjacent house for those same
five decades**, the two families "super tight for fucking ever." Dan's
mother dated Annie's father in high school; two generations of entanglement
were built across this property line before Dan and Annie ever met.

The house is a "golf course house" in the literal sense — its position on
the UCC fairway was the physical seat of Fran's decades of dominance there
(the 1969 hole-in-one, the Women's Crown). Her standing directive was to
stay in it "as long as humanly possible," and the caregiving arrangement
with Dan and Annie — themselves living at the adjacent
[[wiki/places/155-virginia-ave|155 Virginia Ave]] — was built to honor it.
She left it only after the March 2018 fall
([[wiki/timeline/events/fran-death-vigil]]), and the record frames the
two-week nursing-home collapse that followed as the confirmation that the
house had been what was keeping her alive.

The house was actively re-listed after Fran's death; realtor
[[wiki/people/danny-matthews|Danny Matthews]] (also a family acquaintance
through Suz) reported it "finally went pending" on 2019-07-11, with an
aside ("second time's a charm") implying an earlier listing attempt had
fallen through sometime in the prior fifteen months.

The Ulmer grandparents are confirmed as Jim and Sugie: a March 2018
message from [[wiki/people/ellen-ulmer|Ellen Ulmer]] (Annie's mother)
places herself "at Jim and Sugie's" and relays that Sugie was separately
looking for caregiving help of her own, suggesting some coordination
between the two neighboring households' care arrangements during Fran's
final months.
