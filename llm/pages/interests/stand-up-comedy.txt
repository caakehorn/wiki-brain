# Stand-Up Comedy

Stand-up is not a passive favorite for Dan — it is one of the few live
art forms he physically chased, an art he tried to *make* himself, and a
grammar that leaks straight into how he talks (see [[wiki/mind/profile/linguistic-profile]]
and the O&A/Opie radio saturation on [[wiki/self/youtube-watch-history]]).
The masterlist and the message dump together show three layers: a heavy
2019 New York club-going run, an actual ambition to get on stage, and a
household ritual of driving to filmed specials.

## The 2019 New York run — a real itinerary, not a taste

Like his teen concerts ([[wiki/timeline/events/teen-concert-years]]),
Dan kept a dated list of the comics he saw live. It clusters almost
entirely in the autumn of 2019, the tail of the New York years, and reads
as a person working a room of the city's alt/dirtbag stand-up scene night
after night:

| Date | Comedian(s) seen live |
|------|-----------------------|
| 2019-04-29 | Chris DiStefano |
| 2019-10-29 | Colin Quinn |
| 2019-10-30 | Dan Soder, Mark Normand, Rich Vos, Shane Gillis, Pete Lee, Jim Gaffigan |
| 2019-11-05 | Tim Dillon, Ian Fidance |
| 2019-11-07 | Bobby Kelly, Big Jay Oakerson |
| 2019-11-21 | Aaron Berg, Yamaneika Saunders, Sean Patton |

Also named as favorites without a logged date: Dave Attell, Ron
Bennington, Andrew Schulz. The roster is telling — this is the Legion of
Skanks / Comedy Cellar / Stand Up NY orbit, the podcast-fed New York
club circuit, not arena comedy. The single night of 2019-10-30 alone
(six comics including Gaffigan, Normand, Gillis and Soder) is the shape
of someone going to a specific room and catching the whole lineup.

## He tried to do it himself

The ambition to perform, not just watch, is documented across years:

- **2017-12-28:** "One thing I'm going to force myself to do in 2018 is
  try stand up at least once," followed immediately by "I'm gonna ask
  Vanessa where the open mic was she went to and try to get a couple
  minutes of material together."
- **2018-03-09:** "well...until i start doing stand up" — the plan still
  alive months later.
- **2019-04-12:** "im just trying to develop a solid 5 minutes because
  thats usually what you get at an open mic" — the concrete craft goal:
  the five-minute open-mic set.
- **2019-02-19**, a friend: "Yesterday I literally had the thought of
  you doing stand up and using that story in a bit I swear I did."
- **2019-04-02**, the same friend hands him a stage name: "Dude I came
  up with your comedy name for when u do standup/YouTube. It's **'Nad
  Knarf'**" — "Dan Frank" reversed. Whether or not a set ever happened,
  the persona got as far as a name.

This fits the broader self-model: the same recursive, hyper-verbal,
gallows-humor instinct that shows up as "black irony as a psychic
ventilator" in the profile is the instinct that wanted a microphone.

## The specials-as-ritual layer

Comedy was also a family/relationship outing, not only a solo NYC thing:

- **2018-12-03**, to his mother Suzanne: "We're going to Philadelphia
  for a taping of a standup special" — a road trip to sit in a filmed
  taping audience. [[wiki/people/bobby-cole|Bobby Cole's]] thread
  identifies the special: a **Chip Chipperson** taping, invited by
  Bobby ("Nana cumia will be there") and attended December 8 — Dan
  privately called it "pretty bad" despite the 9+ hour round trip.
- **2019-10-02**, to Bobby Cole, the corpus's only direct evidence of a
  *completed* open-mic set: a bit about "a hooker using my credit card
  to cut a line of coke" that drew groans before he could finish the
  word "escort." The same message names his motivation ("rich vos AND
  vic henley can do this") and his own blunt read on the material: "all
  of my material is just me saying a bunch of ways 'i am a drug addict
  and a failure in most endeavors.'"
- The recurring want to drag people to shows: **2019-04-15**, arranging
  a return to the city "before 6:30pm tomorrow to go to a comedy show. i
  already got a third ticket for you." When that ask became a fight over
  Annie's realtor workload, his reply is a compact sample of his sarcasm
  register: "youre right..a 1 hour comedy show that costs $7 a piece is
  very destructive."

## Why it matters

The stand-up thread ties three already-documented things together: the
years of Opie & Anthony / radio-comedy immersion on
[[wiki/self/youtube-watch-history]], the sitcom-idiom borrowing on
[[wiki/interests/the-office]], and the linguistic self-portrait — a person
whose default mode is bit-shaped, timing-aware, and built for a room. It
is one more case of a real, lived pursuit (he went to the shows, he wrote
five minutes, he had a stage name) that the wiki had only implied through
adjacent pages.

**Gaps:** the 2019 club dates come from the "Favorite Comedians"
attendance list in Dan Profile.txt; venue names are not attached to each
date there. The full text and date of the open-mic bit's set beyond the
one line recounted to Bobby Cole is not otherwise recorded.
