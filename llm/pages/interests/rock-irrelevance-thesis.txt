# The 2nd Most Famous 'Jimmy Pop' in Pennsylvania

**Source:** ChatGPT conversation on music/cultural trends (chatgpt.com/c/68717208-4930-8002-a5de-8f79fe2dddee)

**Core Argument (Dan):** Current era is the first time since ~1963 that rock and roll (and anything guitar + drum kit) is completely irrelevant to popular music trends. Guitar-driven music is now "automatically lame" in mainstream.

## Supporting Data (ChatGPT-provided)

**Streaming:**
- Rock: ~234B streams (US)
- Hip-hop/R&B: 341B streams (US)
- Pop overshadowing rock

**Market Share:**
- Rock: 11% US streams
- Hip-hop: 31%

**Charts & Playlists:**
- Guitar-driven tunes nearly invisible on current charts.
- Biggest "rock" acts are catalog assets, not new breaks.

**Instrument Sales:**
- Guitars halved in last decade.
- Fewer young players.

**Cultural Shift:**
- Seismic / geological generational change.
- From 1963–~2000s: rock = unbroken center of gravity (Beatles through Nirvana, pop borrowing rock aesthetics).
- Now: rock = vintage museum piece.
- Mainstream goes rap or synth-pop, not axe solos.

**Probability estimate (per source):** >90% that rock/guitar is currently irrelevant in mainstream pop culture and streaming metrics.

**Caveat noted:** Do not overstate — catalog rock still exists as legacy, but new guitar music not breaking culturally.

## Relation to Favorites & Music Identity

- Strongly aligns with [[wiki/interests/favorites/music]] profile: heavy electronic/bass, hip-hop/rap, emo/post-hardcore clusters (JPEGMAFIA, Kanye, LYNY, Knock2, My Chemical Romance, Fall Out Boy, etc.).
- [[wiki/interests/music/concepts/sub-bass-signature]]: 63–85% across all aliases (SLOPPP → MOGZART → GRIPNOTIC) — the authentic curatorial voice.
- Rejection of guitar rock as dominant is consistent with production focus on maximalist electronic, bass, non-guitar textures.
- Ties to [[wiki/self/context-core]] §5: "Music — the one sincere, self-assembling thread." Shipping/public exposure is bottleneck, not taste or capacity.

## Broader Context

This is not casual taste preference. It is observation of structural cultural shift used to defend current artistic direction against "guitar is still cool" pushback.
