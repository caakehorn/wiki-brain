# Knock2

Los Angeles-based bass house producer (real name Alex Coba) who broke
into the genre's front rank in the mid-2020s largely through Skrillex
collaborations and remix work, helping define the current wave of
maximalist, vocal-sample-forward bass house that dominates festival main
stages as of 2025–2026 — the exact scene and moment GRIPNOTIC's own
revival output is positioning itself within.

## In Favorites

Six tracks, all "SPOTIFY LIKED 2025-2026": "dashstar* – VIP," "ready 2,"
"dashstar*," "Make U SWEAT!," "2HEARTs," "feel U luv Me." The presence of
both a track and its VIP (an alternate, usually more intense club remix
of the same source material) reflects a specific kind of engaged
listening — not just liking a song, but following how a producer reworks
and re-releases their own material, which is closer to studying a
technique than consuming a playlist.

## Genre placement and timing

Knock2's rise (2024–2026) tracks almost exactly with the period the
[[wiki/interests/music/aliases/gripnotic|GRIPNOTIC]] page identifies as
Dan's own production "reactivation" — meaning this isn't legacy taste
carried over from an earlier era, it's live engagement with the artist
currently setting the pace in the exact lane GRIPNOTIC operates in. That
timing makes Knock2 a plausible direct reference point for current
production decisions, not just a listening preference running parallel
to the work.

## Connection to the sub-bass signature

Like [[wiki/interests/favorites/music/artists/lyny]], Knock2's tracks are
built around aggressive, vocal-chop-driven low end rather than melodic or
lyrical content — see [[wiki/interests/music/concepts/lyrics-as-timbre]]
for why that matters here less as a caveat and more as a structural fit:
this is a genre where the vocal fragments function as pure rhythmic and
textural material by design, for every listener, not just for Dan. The
involuntary sub-bass signature documented across all four of Dan's own
aliases ([[wiki/interests/music/concepts/sub-bass-signature]]) makes
Knock2's whole catalog close to a native-frequency match rather than an
adjacent-genre appreciation.
