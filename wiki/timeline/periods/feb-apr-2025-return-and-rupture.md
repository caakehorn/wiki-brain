# Feb–April 2025: Return and Rupture

The immediate aftermath of the January 2025 affair discovery and the
February 22, 2025 forced exit from the NYC apartment — the specific weeks
the broader [[wiki/timeline/periods/2025-collapse]] page names only in
aggregate. Source is a ChatGPT conversation Dan ran on April 27, 2025,
asking the model to act as "an exclusively impartial arbiter of the fact
patterns" over a ten-day message log (April 17–26) between him and
[[wiki/people/annie-ulmer|Annie]] — a self-authored, contemporaneous
analysis distinct from the later raw-CSV recounts that
[[wiki/mind/synthesis/dan-annie-fallout-verdict]] performs on the Aug
2025–Mar 2026 terminal phase. This page fills the four-month gap between
the two.

## The exit and the asymmetric landing

After the affair came to light, the NYC apartment was put on a "shot
clock" — Dan describes roughly $10,000 already owed plus a $7,000 ConEd
bill, financial realities he says made continuing to argue about staying
in New York "not really even worth the time it would take to debate."
His own account is that he never treated the affair as a "terminal blow"
to the relationship: he proposed returning to Pennsylvania to regroup,
funded by family money, and deliberately left the destination of a new
apartment "intentionally empty" — offering to let Annie pick anywhere and
to find the money to make it happen. Annie instead made a unilateral
decision, without discussing it with Dan first, to move in with her own
parents, while insisting this represented no change in the relationship's
status. Dan landed alone at his mother's house.

That landing was itself precarious: Suzanne Frank had declared personal
bankruptcy in 2024, and the childhood home — [[wiki/people/suzanne-frank|Suz]]'s
house, the one Dan describes as "the single 'safe' place that existed in
my life that i could crash land in" — was listed for sale the same week
this conversation was written (a Thursday in late April 2025). This is a
full year earlier than the sale process [[wiki/people/suzanne-frank]]'s
existing housing-arc section documents (the 337 Saratoga Drive sale to
buyers Jennifer J. and Scott P. Delverm, contingent on *their* bankruptcy
approval, closing around June 2026) — the same house evidently went
through at least two separate listing attempts across 2025 and 2026
before the eventual sale, with Suz's own 2024 bankruptcy as the
previously undocumented first driver.

## The week in miniature: April 17–25, 2025

Dan supplied a day-by-day account of a single representative week,
asking the model to timeline it. The pattern that emerges is a tight,
repeating cycle: drug-procurement logistics (a supplier called "Bop," a
contact named John, [[wiki/people/suzanne-frank|Suz]] often facilitating
delivery) generate friction over timing and money; Annie's caregiving
obligations — for a grandmother-figure referred to as "Sugie," plus
church and extended family — repeatedly compress the time available for
the relationship itself; Dan reads each compression as evidence Annie no
longer prioritizes him and periodically escalates to statements like "I
just want to know once and for all who I spent 10 years with"; short
repair cycles follow (an evening together, an apology, "I love you"),
resetting the loop rather than resolving it. Annie's own language in the
log names the caregiving load directly: "dealing with an old women's
piss," feeling like "a slave" and, elsewhere, "a servant."

Two specific frictions stand out as more than routine: on April 21, a
playful use of an AI Pixar-style photo filter turned into a boundary
violation when Dan continued uploading and sharing Annie's images —
including one with a third woman — after she expressed discomfort, and
he registers her objection but does not stop. On April 22, Dan explicitly
names his own depression and pushes Annie away ("leave me alone"); she
complies, then returns unprompted later that day and stays until 10 PM,
which he logs as a genuine repair. On April 24, Dan states the core
problem in the plainest terms found on record for this window: not
chores, not logistics, but that the relationship itself feels
irreparably "smashed" with no visible commitment to a future from Annie's
side.

## Reading it against the rest of the corpus

This week-level granularity is not available anywhere else in the wiki
for the Feb–April 2025 window; the existing 2025-collapse period page
covers the year at a much coarser grain, and the fully quantified
terminal-phase record on dan-annie-fallout-verdict.md begins in August
2025. What this page adds is the *shape* of the intervening months: the
same pursuit/withdrawal pattern the later terminal-phase statistics
measure numerically (74/17/11 abuse triad, the reassurance-dominance
triad, the volume asymmetry) is already fully present here in April,
four months earlier and with Dan generating the analysis himself rather
than the wiki reconstructing it after the fact — evidence the pattern was
stable across the whole terminal window, not something that developed
gradually over 2025.
