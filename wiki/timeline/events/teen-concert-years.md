# Teen Concert Years (2005–2008)

The stretch of Dan's life, roughly age 16 to 19, when live music was a
primary mode of identity rather than a streaming background. This is not
inferred from a favorites list — it is a literal itinerary he kept. A
Facebook "Concerts I've gone to" note, preserved verbatim across the
corpus (Dan Profile.txt, the Gemini Activity export, and the failed-wiki
log), records the shows he routed himself to across Ohio, Pennsylvania,
New York, Maryland, and New Jersey during the mid-2000s emo/pop-punk
boom.

|------|-----------|--------------|

The 2007 Facebook status layer corroborates the intensity of that year:
posts read "swooning over the new FOB," "in New York City to see Fall Out
Boy at TRL," and a Say Anything obsession — the same window the profile
names as the "romantic collapse fantasy" imprinting period
([[wiki/mind/psychosexual/developmental-origins]]). The same status layer
names "slaying on halo" and Super Mario Galaxy in the same breath — the
concert devotion and [[wiki/interests/video-games|gaming]] were
coordinates of one identity cluster, not separate hobbies.

## What it shows

Three things are true at once here that the wiki had flattened into "top
music artists":

1. **He toured the scene, he didn't just like it.** Three Fall Out Boy
   shows in eleven months plus a release-day television taping is fandom
   as logistics, not a playlist.
2. **The TRL taping was an industry gimmick he was physically inside of.**
   The Feb 6 2007 appearance was the 3-city / 3-show *Infinity On High*
   release-week stunt; he was in the studio for it.
3. **The secret show was a deliberate in-joke he was let in on.** Buffalo
   was billed "Schrute Farms" (a *The Office* reference — see
   [[wiki/interests/the-office]]) and, per a 2025 ChatGPT cross-check he
   ran himself, was the first and only time *Take This To Your Grave* was
   played in full.

This is the same collector's-completeness instinct that shows up later in
the message-corpora forensics and the wiki itself: a teenager who logs
every show he attends and remembers the fake name on the marquee is
already running the archival reflex the rest of the corpus documents.
