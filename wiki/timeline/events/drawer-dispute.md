**Type:** Workplace cash variance / power dynamic conflict

**Outcome:** Termination (per [[wiki/self/context-core]] §5: "BFS termination (cash-variance dispute)")

## What Happened

During a 5-11 shift (bumped to 8), assistant manager (AM) pulls Dan aside post-shift claim:

"Your drawer was $50 short last night. I paid it out of my own pocket, you owe me."

Core contradiction (identified immediately in chat):
- CSR closing report at time of close: "nothing over or under".
- Next day AM claim: $50 short, personal payout.

## Immediate Forensic Breakdown (from source chat)

**Procedural Tell:**
- "I paid it out of my own pocket, you owe me" is not standard accounting procedure at any business with functioning controls.
- Real process: documented upward, paper trail, signed acknowledgement, escalation thresholds.
- Personal absorption + verbal collection from employee = no employer-side record (laundering feature, not bug).

**Legal Note (PA):**
- Employers cannot deduct from wages for shortages without specific written authorization.
- Even authorized deductions cannot take employee below minimum wage.
- Off-books cash demand bypasses all payroll protections.

**Tactical Response (locked in chat):**
- Do not pay (cash or future). Payment = retroactive validation + signals "this works".
- Escalate only to owner/GM/corporate with documentation.
- If AM refuses formal channel: tells you everything.

**Facts to Lock Down (explicit list in source):**
- Business structure (franchise/chain/family?).

- Direct observation of CSR report vs hearsay.
- Cameras over register/safe.
- Who recounted, when, witnessed.
- Prior identical claims to other staff.
- Schedule change (5-11 → 8) connection.
