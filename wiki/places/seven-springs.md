# Seven Springs

Seven Springs is a ski resort about 25 miles from Uniontown that
functioned as Dan's principal social world every weekend throughout his
adolescence — not a vacation destination but the recurring anchor of
his actual peer group, distinct from and reportedly stronger than the
friendships he had at his own high school (Laurel Highlands). The
family kept a condo there, unit K2 in "The Villages" at Sunridge, and
the weekly rhythm of driving up, skiing, and returning is named in his
own cognitive-profile notes as the formative social structure of his
childhood — alongside trampoline training kept up specifically as
off-season ski-season prep.

## The first drug exposure cohort

The Seven Springs ski crew is explicitly identified, in Dan's own
retrospective account, as his first drug-exposure cohort — and the
timeline is notable: his first cocaine use is dated to 2004, in the
context of that crew, a full year *before* the November 2005 hinge
event (his father's rehab disclosure, his parents' divorce) that the
rest of the corpus treats as the inciting rupture for the family's
addiction narrative. Read against that later event, Seven Springs
complicates a clean "the divorce caused it" story: the peer-level
exposure was already underway before the parental-level crisis that
gets narratively credited with starting it. Dan began dating Danielle,
his first documented girlfriend, the same year (2004), while attending
Laurel Highlands as Danielle attended Uniontown High School.

## Regional context

Seven Springs and the neighboring Liberty resort sit inside a small but
real corner of national freeskiing culture — Tom Wallisch, one of the
sport's defining 2007–2012 stars (the "pretzel," the SuperUnknown
video-contest breakout, the 4Bi9 Media era), came up through this exact
regional scene, a detail Dan has separately researched and discussed at
length with an AI as adult interest rather than personal connection —
he isn't described as having known Wallisch, but the shared home turf
gives his own ski years a specific cultural backdrop rather than a
generic one.
