# Video Games

Gaming is not a rated favorites category in the masterlist, and the wiki
had no page for it — but it recurs across the corpus as a real, if
background, thread: a teen signal in the 2007 Facebook layer, a concrete
ownership-and-ban saga in the 2016–2018 messages, and a literal
liquidation event in 2025. The meaning is "a long-running low-grade
presence," not "a passion," and the wiki had been treating it as neither.

## The teen signal (2007)

The 2007 Facebook status layer names gaming as part of the raw
pre-armor self-presentation: posts reference "slaying on halo" and
"Super Mario Galaxy" in the same breath as "swooning over the new FOB" and
"in New York City to see Fall Out Boy at TRL." Gaming was one coordinate
of the teenage identity cluster, not a separate hobby.

## The PlayStation years (2016–2018)

The message corpus shows an actual console life, including friction:
- "My PlayStation came" (2016-11-30) and "Is the PlayStation working?"
  (2016-12-05) — active ownership.
- "My fucking PlayStation account is banned" (2016-12-10) — the account
  was banned, a notable event worth narrating to the other person.
- "Tim is so much easier to deal with when I play video games with him"
  (2016-12-29) — gaming as a social-regulator with a specific person.
- "I went to get the PlayStation and drop the sub here" (2017-12-08) —
  the console moving between households.
- "And there's probably naked girls and video games and phones and all
  the fun things" (2018-12-21) — gaming among the anticipated contents of
  a space.

## The 2025 liquidation

The thread resurfaces as a material event in late 2025: Dan texts his
mother "i'm so annoyed my mom now wants to go sell fucking old video
games and i have told her so many times" (2025-09-04), and a separate
2025 message searches "is there any market for empty Wii and Xbox cases
that include the instruction booklet." The Gemini activity export
preserves a full games-collection ledger — NHL 07–11, Rockband 2, Tiger
Woods PGA Tour (multiple years), Super Smash Bros. Brawl, Grand Theft
Auto: San Andreas, and more, with per-title resale valuations. The arc is
teen-ownership → adult disinterest → forced divestment of a physical
back-catalog he had told his mother repeatedly not to sell.

## Why this belongs

Another "unutilized for its actual purpose" gap: gaming carries no
favorites rating and no page, yet it is a verifiable through-line from
2007 ("slaying on halo") to 2025 (the Wii/Xbox case liquidation). The
facts are concrete and the emotional register is consistent — low-grade,
long-running, occasionally friction-bearing, finally relinquished under
family pressure. It earns a page.
