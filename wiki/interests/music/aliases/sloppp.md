# Sloppp

## Identity

**SLOPPP** was Dan Frank's early exploratory production alias, active approximately 2013–2014 following the return from NYC to Uniontown. It predates the more stylistically defined MOGZART and GRIPNOTIC phases. Status is `closed`. The triple-P spelling signals the early aesthetic register — irreverent, lo-fi aspirational, not yet a consolidated identity.

## Genre & Style

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Primary genre | House / experimental bass (early) |
| Era | ~2013–2014 (return to Uniontown post-NYC round 1) |
| Sub-bass range | 63–85% (involuntary signature, same as later aliases) |
| Differentiator | Exploratory / developmental; less stylistically defined than MOGZART |
| Status | Fully archived; no revival signals in sources |

## Narrative

SLOPPP is the earliest production alias with a confirmed name in the context-core timeline. It emerges after the NYC studio-work era (2010–2013), when Dan returned to SW Pennsylvania with production skills but without a settled creative identity. The alias tracks with the early period of genre exploration before the production voice consolidated into the bass house / tech house direction that defines MOGZART and GRIPNOTIC.

The involuntary sub-bass signature was already present — it shows up across all alias periods, confirming the 63–85% figure is architectural rather than a later style choice.
