# Elliott Smith

Portland singer-songwriter, 1969–2003. Recorded on four-track cassette
recorders and later in professional studios, but with a production
sensibility that always kept the room quiet enough to hear the breath.
Known for *Either/Or* (1997), *XO* (1998), and *Figure 8* (2000) — albums
of extraordinary melodic precision built around the kind of personal
devastation that sounds like it was written in the dark and shouldn't be
overheard.

He died of two stab wounds to the chest in 2003. The circumstances remain disputed.

## In Favorites

7 tracks in the masterlist — "Waltz #2 (XO)," "King's Crossing," "High
Times," "Whatever (Folk Song in C)," plus remastered repeats — and the
Gemini activity export ranks him the #1 individual artist in a listening
profile otherwise dominated by bass-heavy electronic warfare. That
placement is not a footnote; it is the single clearest data point for the
"two-mode" emotional architecture the profile describes (a hard electronic
shell protecting a hypersensitive core, no stable middle).

## "patron saint of liars and fakes" — the 2013 handle

The most concrete trace of what Smith meant is a 2013 Facebook username:
**"patron saint of liars and fakes."** That phrase is Smith's own
self-description, lifted as an identity. It reveals the specific angle of
the attraction — not sadness as sadness, but Smith's exactitude about
self-deception, about the gap between the presented self and the thing
underneath. For an INTP 5w4 whose default mode is forensic analysis, music
this emotionally precise is the same mode applied to a different domain:
Smith doesn't vague-gesture at feeling, he finds the specific image, the
specific chord, the specific vocal break. The attraction is to the
precision, and to the permission to name the lie plainly.

## The R&B-before-1979 gate (2025)

The attachment shows up as a live filter, not just a nostalgic entry. In a
2025 message laying down a rule for what music is allowed in a shared
space, Dan wrote: "if she insists on playing r&b it has to be before 1979
or it will be overproduced trash… you cut that shit out and go back to
listening to real music like elliott smith or radiohead" (2025-09-02).
Smith is the positive pole of the gate — the artist you pivot *to* when the
overproduced stuff gets cut. The fact that he is the named alternative to
"trash" tells you his position in the listening hierarchy: not a guilty
pleasure but the standard.

## Why this artist fits the profile

Smith is the emotional pole of a library that also includes JPEGMAFIA and
My Chemical Romance — the quietest, most intimate presence in a collection
that elsewhere tends toward intensity. The 7 tracks aren't background
listening; they're what you put on when the volume of everything else gets
too high. The Gemini export frames this as the "glitch that reveals the
system's architecture": the patron saint of whispered ruin sitting at the
top of a bass-music playlist is the visible seam between radical
dissociation (the machine) and radical vulnerability (the wound). There is
no "chill" state in between, and Smith is the evidence for the vulnerable
end.

For the wider self model, this is the same move documented in
[[wiki/mind/profile/deviance-mapping]] — shame transmuted into intellectual
weaponry rather than avoided. Smith names his own defects with clinical
precision and makes that the art; the profile's owner does something
adjacent with his own foresight and betrayal material.

## The Quiet Anchor

In the broader favorites map — JPEGMAFIA's abrasion, MCR's theatrical
volume, the emo cluster's collective emotional intensity — Elliott Smith
functions as the proof that the emotional range extends all the way in the
other direction. The library isn't just loud; it can hold its breath.
