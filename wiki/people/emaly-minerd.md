# Emaly Minerd

Emaly Minerd was a relationship that ran in three separate bursts across more
than two years — a five-month intensive chapter in late 2018 / early 2019
(613 messages, `+172****5006`), a brief and ugly August 2019 revival, and a
September 2020 Facebook coda — all of it orbiting the same axis: a drug-
sourcing contact who tried, repeatedly, to become a real friend, and who
Dan repeatedly pulled back toward "sex or nothing." It is one of the most
emotionally dense threads in the corpus outside the primary relationships,
and it is also one of the clearest outside-view mirrors of Dan's own stated
trajectory: Emaly ends the January 2019 fight by telling him, plainly, "you'll
probably be dead in the next 15 years… living the dream" — a warning that
lands because she is not wrong about the shape of his life, only about the
timeline.

## Who she was

Emaly managed a cell phone store, had a chronic illness requiring periodic
transfusions (framed elsewhere in the corpus as narcolepsy-adjacent), and was
three years into a relationship with a boyfriend, Derek, that she described as
loving but strained — both of them, by her account, wanting things from the
relationship the other couldn't provide. She had written a book she was proud
of (referred to in the January 2019 fight as "stamp notes," self-published,
printed on an HP Officejet, and the subject of an attempted publishing deal
that fell through after a legal threat: "I tried to get it published, I was
threatened to get sued [over the] funding"). She had a son she described as
"actually a genius… he has a drug problem" — the one piece of her own life
that let her see Dan's clearly. The two apparently attended the same high
school and may have crossed paths years earlier ("wish you would have said
that to me in '05 or '06"). She is from the Uniontown area and, like Dan,
had not left it for long.

## Chapter 1 — the five-month arc (September 2018 – January 2019)

### Transactional opening

The relationship opens purely transactional: Emaly buying Adderall, Xanax,
kratom, and occasionally cocaine from Dan, negotiating prices, complaining
about a dealer named Kevin Santore who wasted her time repeatedly. The first
corpus trace of money movement is a Cash App note from October 26, 2018 —
"Emaly Minerd just accepted the $50 payment you sent today" — and she is
already a node in his referral economy: the next day he texts Annie "Lololol
shout out Emaly with the callback" and, tellingly, "What would one Emaly need
a tool built for a real ass man for" — the arrangement's mechanics surfacing
in real time.

### The friendship turn

Within weeks the tone shifts: long, unguarded late-night conversations,
personal disclosures (her illness, her relationship history, a near-breakup
with Derek), and mutual flattery. Emaly tells Dan he reads as "a nice guy, and
dare I even say sweet" despite his own self-description as "a 1% dirtbag" — a
rare moment of someone outside the inner circle taking his self-deprecation
at face value rather than as armor. By November 3, 2018, Dan is telling Annie
"I'm surprised honestly that you don't feel much better after seeing my convo
with Emaly. Like, even when I'm trying to fuck other girls I'm talking you up
and saying how great we are together" — the arrangement's reassurance function
running even mid-seduction.

### The in-person window and the arrangement

By late November 2018 the relationship becomes in-person. Emaly meets both Dan
and [[wiki/people/annie-ulmer|Annie]] together, likes Annie ("she's definitely
one of the rare ones… I've never been able to have many friends that are
girls"), and the three hang out repeatedly. She is aware of and explicitly
discusses the Dan/Annie non-monogamous arrangement, asking pointed questions
about how it works and whether it requires Annie to keep sleeping with other
men for Dan to feel the way he does about her — one of the few outside voices
in the corpus to name the arrangement's mechanics back to Dan directly. On
November 28, 2018, Dan gives her the fullest retrospective account anywhere in
the corpus of how his then-current arrangement with [[wiki/people/alexis-armel|Alexis]]
began — including the detail that he'd "initially" offered Alexis $3,000 to be
"a plaything for Annie" — while also telling Emaly the two women's actual
connection came together on its own. Full account at
[[wiki/timeline/events/annie-alexis-reunion-november-2018]]. That same day
Dan tells Annie "I MAY be able to pull the rabbit out of the hat with that girl
Emaly. I mean I've been striking out consistently with every effort I make, but
I'm getting closer" — the seduction and the arrangement narration happening on
parallel tracks.

### The in-person window (November 27–29, 2018)

The three days Emaly and Dan spend in person — almost entirely at 155 Virginia
Avenue, the apartment Dan shares with Annie — are the emotional core of the
whole relationship, and the corpus preserves them in near-real time. They begin
with a drug drop and end with Emaly reading the room better than Dan does.

**November 27 — the drop and the disclosure.** It opens as a transaction: Dan
texts "Hey I just got the call that there's some adderall around… and even
though I'm still not approved to see you naked… I thought I'd look out for ya"
(emphasis his), then prices the deal as a joke — "Ummmmm I think it comes out
to: 2 underwear shots and 1 full frontal." Emaly sends the photos. He drives to
her store, hands over the pills, and stays. In person she tells him, plainly,
that his self-description is wrong: "all the things you said about you being the
worst type of person, a 1% dirtbag… I'm not buying it at all. I'm a pretty good
people reader… you are a nice guy, and dare I even say sweet." She notes his
demeanor "in person is a lot different than how I would have imagined" and that
his "confidence level fluctuates soooo much from text to in person." He tells
her he remembers "being in 8th grade and literally coming up with a plan to get
you to like me" — the high-school overlap made literal: she replies "Wish you
would have said that to me in '05 or '06… my heart woulda stopped for a dead
minute," and notes her boyfriend Derek "would shatter into pieces if he knew I
said that." She reads him as a "good vibe" and a "witch" whose "spell" is
working. The visit ends with him running pills out to her car while Annie,
inside, texts "have fun don't forget to pull out."

**November 28 — the arrangement, narrated.** The next day Emaly comes over
again and the conversation turns into the clearest outside exposition of the
Dan/Annie/Alexis structure anywhere in the corpus. She asks directly how it
works; Dan answers in full, including the origin line: "Initially I offered her
[Alexis] $3000 to be a plaything for Annie… But it worked out even better
because the first time they got together they must have had some kind of
connection." He explains the engine — "Annie is the worlds coolest girlfriend
for me. There's nobody I could have sex with that, as long as I'm honest and
upfront, she would be upset about" — and the jealousy-as-currency frame: "this
is all stuff we had to spell out and figure out what our rules would be… but it
also only works because we both are obsessed with sex. I would go nuts if I
didn't get to knock the bottom out at least once a day." Emaly, strikingly,
*gets* it: "You guys use the basic obstacles and struggles in relationships in
a way people could never handle… instead of trying to fight against something
constantly… human instinct, you guys just let nature take its course." She
gives the arrangement "some serious relationship bonus points for that kind of
communication." It is the one moment in the corpus where an outside woman
treats the arrangement as a philosophy worth taking seriously rather than as a
red flag — and Dan, for his part, tells her "I've never ever been this honest in
a relationship before."

**The "vibe" and the warning Dan misses.** That same night Emaly delivers the
observation that should have mattered: sitting in the apartment, she texts "I
don't think your girlfriend is very fond of me, or fond of you hanging with me…
there's something about it that she doesn't like." Dan's answer is revealing of
his own architecture — "I know I have a hard time being super pleasant around
guys that she is fucking… jealousy is like a currency in our relationship… as
long as we both know how we feel about each other on a long term basis and
maintain complete honesty, the jealousy can become fun." He reassures her there
is "not any real animosity there," but her read was the accurate one; the
January collapse is the proof. The window also surfaces the Kevin Santore
saga — Emaly's four-try ordeal with a dealer who stood her up twice, sent her
to "the shitty projects in Brownsville," and wasted "more than anything in the
world" her time — which is why she keeps coming back to Dan: he is the reliable
source, the one who "got people trying to sell you shit left and right like
everyday" while she has to "literally hunt that kind of shit down."

**November 29 — the high-school origin, stated outright.** The last morning of
the window, Emaly names the through-line of the whole relationship: "It's
interesting how things turn out… I can still remember sitting in music class
with you, sitting in the back. When the bell rang you were the first one out.
Like lightning. I didn't stand a chance trying to catch up." And: "I never in my
wildest dreams could have ever imagined us talking when we turned 30, sitting
in a car doing lines." Dan: "at least we aren't fucking lame… at least we
aren't fucking rubes." She had, the night before, been stood up by Derek
("my boyfriend is an asshole and completely blew me off tonight") — the same
pattern Dan will later enact on her in 2020, inverted.

### The refusal and the first collapse (December 2018 – January 7, 2019)

On December 6, 2018, Emaly draws an explicit line: she won't be "the kinky
pawn" the couple needs to fulfill their relationship, tells Dan the only way
she seems able to stay connected to him is through sex, and says she won't do
that — she's put too many years into her relationship with Derek to risk it for
someone who "just wants sex." She offers friendship on those terms and asks
whether that's possible.

The answer, over the following weeks, is functionally no. A month-long silence
(December 6 to January 7) breaks with Emaly reaching out again, saying she'd
kept her distance out of respect for Dan and Annie but missed the friendship.
Dan's reply — reconstructed from her reaction — makes clear there's no room in
his life for "friends who aren't hookups." She calls it out directly and sadly:
"Everyone needs emotional connection… you don't want to be friends with me
because of how I look." She writes that she'd almost left Derek at one point and
would have told Dan if it had happened — a disclosure that goes nowhere.

### The January 27, 2019 fight (full reconstruction)

The final exchange is the relationship's ugliest and most honest moment, and
the corpus preserves it almost completely from Dan's side (the export marks
these rows "Sent" with no handle, which is the known sender-side quirk — see
the direction-field caveat below). It is worth reconstructing in full because
it is the clearest single document of Dan's "sex or nothing" filter operating
in real time, and of Emaly refusing to accept it.

It opens gently. Emaly (9:51am): "Hey you. I gotta question" — she wants to
know if he still wants "that copy of stamp notes," then, "could you help me
out, in a purely friendly, -no strings attached- matter lol." If he can't even
talk to her, "it's cool."

Dan's response, at 6:55pm, is a volley of insults disguised as jokes: "Listen
I'm not in the habit of buying or finding drugs, giving money to, or pretending
to want to read books about a passé uninteresting drugs like heroin for girls
I'm not fucking." He pivots to an analogy — "I flew you out for the job
interview, you declined the position. The position is filled and now you want
more free airfare?… The perks only come with the job sis" — confirming she had
visited him in NYC ("the job interview" being a trip he'd funded) and that he
frames the friendship itself as a deferred payment on a sexual role she'd
refused. Of the book: "I'm sure it's… a very insightful… book, well for being
self published and all."

Emaly's reaction is measured at first, then scorching (7:46pm): "Wow. Well, ok.
I wasn't going to ask you for anything. I've never expected handouts… ever in
my life. Not even from a trust fund kid that continuously boasts about how many
zeros are attached to his bank accounts… I hope you have enough cash in your
accounts to buy the people you need. With the terrible character you just
showed me here, you're going to need it." And (7:54pm): "There's literally no
reason why you had to just insult me so badly… At least I have tried to do
something with my life… the opinion of someone who has to pay people for
company instead of a real connection or any substance, well… their opinion
doesn't matter to me."

Dan keeps joking. Late that night the fight runs for three more hours. He
states the rule outright (9:42pm): "Emaly I adore sex and it's a big part of my
life and I don't want to date girls who aren't like way way into fucking."
Then, with surreal self-awareness (10:35pm): "I have VERY little integrity, to
be sure. But the one place I do have integrity in is my belief that humor is
the answer to all." He insists he was "never insulting a book" — "is it really
a book if it's printed on an HP Officejet 4500 laser printer? *rimshot*." He
tells her he could "totally still turn this around if I wanted to," and
half-seriously claims a "big dick electromagnetic frequency" that, if flipped,
would have her "in her car" to him.

Emaly stays sharp throughout. She names the mechanism (10:18pm): "You be a
complete dick, tell me you can't talk to me when your girlfriend doesn't allow
it. And here we are." Dan corrects her — "wrong, I never said she didn't allow
it" — and explains the actual rule (10:19pm): "It's not reassuring to her for an
emotional relationship to exist, certainly without a physical one being
present… We don't care who each other fuck as long as they aren't falling in
love." She sees the loop (10:20pm): "yeah I know I got that… So why are you
talking to me still?" His answer (10:22pm): "Because we both know I could
totally still turn this around if I wanted to."

Then the warning. Emaly (10:39pm), after he mocks her concern for his future:
"You think that's impressive? Set your standards higher kid. Before you end up
broke, a drug addict, in jail, rehab, and alone… you'll probably be dead in the
next 15 years. Living the dream." She names another burnout case, "Matt Caton,"
as a comparable cautionary example ("all the matt catons of the world… think
it's cool"). Dan's reply is the most self-lucid line in the whole corpus on
this subject (10:35pm): "I am a drug addict. I have been a drug addict for a
decade. I think it's rad… I am a scumbag junkie entitled brat." He closes the
warning with "That 'warning' might have been applicable at age 13 for me. So
with all due respect, get fucked."

The fight ends where it began — transactional. Dan (10:45pm): "Check your cash
app don't spend it all in one place," then (10:48pm) offers to "get rid of a
few bars and some adderall" and leave them for her; when she asks why, he
backpedals (10:53pm): "Oh did I say bars and adderall?… Sorry that's only for
my bottom bitchrs." She: "I figured as much." And then, the collapse the whole
five months had been building toward (10:58pm), already having taken Xanax and
"shitty Coke" that night: "You know… I already did some Xanax and well, shitty
Coke tonight, but anyway… what's one harmless fuck." Dan declines, finally
plain (11:17pm): "Actually I do believe all I was looking for was a 'hookup'… I
never said 'fuck'… This is why I'm with Annie… So goodbye and good luck."

Emaly's warning is the line the wiki keeps returning to: not because it was
cruel, but because it was accurate about the *shape* — the drugs, the
isolation, the self-sabotage — while Dan was still young enough (30) that the
"15 years" read as hyperbole. The corpus vindicates the shape, not the date.

## Chapter 2 — the August 2019 revival (nudes and a blocked contact)

Eight months later, Emaly resurfaces as a fallback supply node. On August 2,
2019, Dan tells a friend he "might be getting those subs from emaly tomorrow
rather than teddy" — she is one of two people he can source Suboxone from that
day. That same evening a Cash App request lands: "Emaly Minerd requested $25."
She also shares a Google Photos album with him. Two days later (August 4),
Dan's verdict on the revival is brutal and recorded to a friend, not to her:
"emaly sent me noodz and they were so bad that i literally didn't respond and
blocked her lol." The $25 request lingers as two more Cash App reminders
(August 6 and August 12) — he never answers them. There is no evidence of any
reply to her on his side; the revival dies in a block. This is the cleanest
instance of the pattern Emaly predicted in January: when she is not a hookup,
there is no conversation, only a mute request and a block.

## Chapter 3 — the September 2020 Facebook coda (blown off, again)

The last chapter is a single evening on Facebook Messenger, September 21, 2020,
during the market-era window — and it is almost a parody of the relationship's
structure. Emaly wants to come over; Dan is out and keeps not coming back. The
thread is a wall of her waiting:

- 3:32pm: "I think [I] got a ride" / "And a ride back home"
- 3:33pm: "i;m not ognna be around for a litle bit" / "i;ll hit u when back"
- 3:34pm: "Just bring the shit and meet me, I'll walk. What ya say" — the deal
  is the point; the visit is the excuse.
- 3:39pm: "Today is the worst day ever man I just got some bad news on top of
  being yelled at all day"
- 4:37pm: "I found a ride so let me know when you get back and I'll come over"
- 6:12pm–6:23pm: a run of "Emaly Minerd" / "You missed a call from Emaly" /
  "I would like to hang out tonight" / "You're blowing me off again"
- 6:41pm: "Hey dude what's the deal"
- 6:55pm: "Can u tell me what's up because it's been like 4 hours I've been
  waiting"
- 7:14pm: "You missed a call from Emaly" / "This is exactly why I might
  [rethink] going anywhere with you, I thought that we had plans, you invited
  me over now I'm irritated"
- 7:24pm: "This is fucking rude"
- 7:38pm: "Well, I'm not coming" / "Which really sucks because I've wasted my
  entire day"

He never responds in the captured thread. The same day, elsewhere in the
corpus, he tells Annie "i'm selling some bars to emaly minerd" — so the
September 21 non-meeting was, on his side, a drug sale he'd already moved on
from, conducted over a thread where she was still waiting to be picked up. The
coda inverts Chapter 1: in 2018 she was the one who drew the line and he who
pushed; by 2020 he is the one who simply doesn't show up, and the "friendship"
she'd begged for in January 2019 has become a 4-hour silence she names as rude
to no reply.

## What the three chapters show

Read together, the Emaly thread is a single thesis tested three times: a woman
offers Dan a non-sexual connection; he cannot metabolize it and routes
everything back to the deal. In Chapter 1 she articulates the rule and leaves.
In Chapter 2 she offers her body (the nudes) and gets blocked. In Chapter 3 she
offers patience and gets silence. The through-line is the [[wiki/mind/concepts/contact-gini|contact
concentration]] principle in miniature — every relationship Dan maintains is
either a supply channel or a sexual one; the "friend who isn't a hookup" slot
stays empty by design, not by accident. Emaly is unusual only because she
said the quiet part out loud and survived long enough to be proven right.
