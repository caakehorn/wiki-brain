# Ej Rags

Ej Rags is a Williamsburg, Brooklyn contact — "a block from the
bridge" as of an October 2011 hello — whose one substantive exchange,
July 12, 2013, is really about one thing: Dan's own account of why he
destroys his creative work.

## "That was so I can create something new"

Prompted by seeing Dan's art on Instagram, Ej praises it directly —
"it's really good by the way, like really really good" — and is
visibly startled to learn Dan had thrown it away. Dan's explanation is
stated plainly and without hedging: "i usually throw most stuff i do
away, that was [so] i can create something new." Ej supplies his own
analogy from graffiti culture — "you can't do one thing and look at it,
something else is gonna get painted over it" — and Dan, in the same
conversation, dates the return to Uniontown precisely: "i just moved
back from NYC and i'm super output recently," placing the exchange
inside the opening days of the
[[wiki/timeline/periods/uniontown-return-2013-2015|2013 Uniontown
return]]. An earlier aside in the same exchange — a Myers-Briggs/INTP
question Dan asks Ej, unanswered ("i dont know what that is") — is a
smaller data point alongside the corpus's other early self-typing
instances.

## Vinyl

The rest of the thread is a genuine collector's exchange: Ej deals in
blues and jazz 78s specifically, discusses an unplayed first-pressing
find and a Chess/Howlin' Wolf session detail ("you can hear him get
pissed when they can't play Red Rooster"), and Dan, who says he has
"a couple hundred albums" but collects rather than sells, admits to an
accidental "ton of prog records" he doesn't understand. In passing, Ej
mentions buying silver from Dan's mother and being warned he "should
have taught her a lesson" for not researching the price — one line
about [[wiki/people/suzanne-frank|Suz]] with no other corroboration in
the corpus.
