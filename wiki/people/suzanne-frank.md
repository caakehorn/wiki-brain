# Suz (Suzanne Frank)

Dan's mother and, across four decades, the single most constant structural
presence in his life — the person who has most often been both the floor under
him and the hand on the decision about where he lives. The relationship is best
read as a long practical partnership with two poles that the corpus documents in
the same breath: close day-to-day cooperation (property work, money bridging,
logistics) and sharp boundary-setting that, at moments, redraws the terms of
their arrangement. The corpus records both tones, sometimes within a single day.

## Identity and lineage

Suzanne Whyel Shrum Frank, born September 15, 1962, in Pittsburgh.
GEDCOM-confirmed parents: George Dixon Shrum Jr. (b. 1937) and Rebecca Diane
Van Voorhis (b. 1939). The maternal line runs to
[[wiki/people/fran-coldren|Fran Coldren]], the great-grandmother Dan names as
his biggest life influence — placing Suz on the Appalachian pole of the
[[wiki/mind/synthesis/ancestral-dialectic|ancestral binary]], the side against
which Rick's paternal Ashkenazi hypervigilance is read. 23andme data
([[wiki/self/ancestry]]) confirms the WV/PA coal-country roots the family
narrative carries.

She divorced [[wiki/people/rick-frank|Rick Frank]] after the 2005 parental
rupture — the hinge event Dan's attachment analyses trace forward through every
later relationship. The record's account of that rupture: an affair surfaced
while Rick was in rehab. Suz's own perspective on it is not in the corpus; the
wiki view is entirely Dan-side, and should be read as one participant's frame.

Mother of Dan and [[wiki/people/vanessa-frank|Vanessa Frank]].

## The realtor and the family house

A working realtor (PA license RS305558, Park Place Realty Group), based at
[[wiki/places/337-saratoga-drive|337 Saratoga Drive]] — the house the family
built in 1996, and Dan's childhood and current home. She previously worked at
Coldwell Banker; per an August 2020 family-thread conversation (found
2026-07-14), a manager there "was really abusing the system and taking
advantage of the agents under him" while, in Dan's read, Suz "knew that and did
nothing." Her move to the new agency that year coincided with her selling more
houses in a single month than in any two-year stretch at the old one — a shift
Dan attributes partly to the 2020 COVID housing-market uptick and partly to her
having left what he calls a "toxic system." The two readings (market timing vs.
escape from a bad workplace) are not mutually exclusive, and the corpus holds
both.

The professional and the personal are not cleanly separable in her life: the
house she sells is also the house her son lives in, and her work as an agent is
the same skill set that organizes the family's housing in real time.

## The financial relationship

The corpus is consistent on one point: Suz has been the family's primary
financial artery across Dan's adult life. Documented instances:

- **2017–2018 (poverty floor / deep cycle):** $750/week borrowing during the
  worst stretch; recurring rescue-and-repayment cycles.
- **The Tom supply pipeline:** Cash App intermediation routing funds through
  her account.
- **The Uniontown "launchpad":** the operating-manual framing is structural —
  her Uniontown property operations function as the base whose equity and
  logistics fund Dan's higher-autonomy digital work. (See
  [[wiki/timeline/periods/2025-collapse]] "Decoupling/Grounding Paradox.")

What the corpus also records, without assigning blame, is the emotional shape
of these cycles: the same hand that bridges a gap is sometimes the one that
later names the terms. The rescue and the reckoning travel together in the
record.

## The housing arc (acute, 2025–2026)

This is the live edge of the relationship as of mid-2026.

**337 Saratoga Drive.** She is selling it to buyers Jennifer J. and Scott P.
Delverm for $465k, contingent on their sale of 19 Jeffries at ≤$229.9k, a
bankruptcy approval, and a 7-day vacate. Dan lives there; no successor plan is
confirmed in the corpus. The sale is the event that ends the childhood-home
chapter and opens an unresolved housing question.

**463 Morgantown Street.** As 337 goes under contract, Suz and Dan are
positioned to land at 463 Morgantown — a move documented as legally unfinished:
no signed lease and no confirmed power of attorney at move-in, owner
[[wiki/people/alexander-jackson|Alexander Jackson]], with Suz operating in a
blended agent / tenant / caretaker capacity that the
[[wiki/legal/463-morgantown|463 file]] notes leaves liability unseparated. The
same file records unresolved property mechanics (a mechanics lien involving
Arnu; incomplete contractor work by [[wiki/people/john-carney|John Carney]]).
Suz's own finances are, per the file, cyclical — the housing question sits
inside a larger pattern of periods of slack and periods of stretch.

The through-line: she is, at once, the person deciding where the family lives
and the person whose own arrangements are themselves in motion. The housing
story is therefore less "a decision about Dan" than "a shared transition two
people are navigating without a fixed destination yet named."

## The Ulmer nexus — a generational loop

From the DANSYNTH record: **Suz dated Annie Ulmer's father in high school**
(late '70s / early '80s). Dan's decade with Annie thus recapitulates a pairing
his mother and Annie's father had already run — one lot away from where both
families' grandparents lived side by side for fifty years
([[wiki/places/117-belmont-circle]]). The genealogy is its own kind of
document: two generations, same small Pennsylvania geography, same two
families. The wiki notes it without claiming it explains anything; it is a
fact the corpus surfaces, offered as context rather than cause.

## The Fran vigil and the keno morning

The sharpest single documented instance of the gap between the family's
*intended* caregiver and its *available* one: on the morning of
[[wiki/timeline/events/fran-death-vigil|Fran's catastrophic fall]], Dan
reached Suz at 8:00 AM. She arrived at 10:00, having played video keno through
the night. The record preserves the two-hour interval as a fixed data point.
The wiki states the interval; the interpretation (what it means about her
reliability, her priorities, the family's care distribution) is left to the
reader, because the corpus contains Dan's reading but not hers.

## The "Suzy" call (forensic node, Jan 2026)

During the post-Annie forensic period, a speakerphone call reached Suz (~Jan 4,
19:00): a drunk Annie asked "I was just at your house, right?" — Suz confirmed;
a male voice said "Suzy"; the call cut in under two seconds. The Gemini-_07
analysis treats the diminutive as a high-entropy social-access key (Annie's own
parents, friends of 40+ years, would say "Suzanne") and the instant hangup as a
non-verbal signal; the associated visitor entered a 10-day blackout after Dan's
staged "wife-warning" screenshot. The vector is isolated from the
[[wiki/people/eli|Eli]] affair — the same [[wiki/mind/concepts/dans-law|Dan's
Law]] discipline of keeping distinct signals distinct applies here as
throughout the terminal-phase forensics. Full analysis:
[[wiki/self/chats/gemini-07]].

## The colonization episode (terminal phase)

The terminal-phase analyses name Suz as the target of a deliberate
image-management effort: Annie maintained a "sweet," attentive persona with her
(wine, help with household tasks) that, in the dossiers' read, converted Dan's
most natural ally into an asset Annie could rely on. By March 2026 Dan was
actively misleading his own mother to protect Annie's cover ("I've been FUCKING
LYING for you. They both think they turned your location back on…", Mar 6
2026) rather than surfacing the deception. The dossiers read this as evidence
of how completely the campaign had taken hold. See
[[wiki/people/annie-ulmer]] and [[wiki/mind/synthesis/attachment-trauma-bond]].

The wiki presents the dossiers' reading as the dossiers' reading — a documented
interpretation of recorded messages, not a settled verdict on Suz's agency
within it.

## How the corpus characterizes her (attributed)

For transparency, the wiki preserves the *corpus's own* shorthand for Suz,
clearly marked as Dan's framing rather than the wiki's claim:

- "Blue MAGA wine mom" — a political-and-domestic thumbnail Dan uses.
- A wine-centered evening rhythm is a recurring motif in the family-thread
  descriptions.
- "oscillating savior↔adversary" — Dan's phrase for the fast flips between
  close partnership and sharp boundary-setting.

These are recorded as the subject's own characterizations in the source
material. The wiki does not independently assert them as clinical or moral
facts; it notes them as the lens through which the corpus's author views his
mother, which is itself data.
