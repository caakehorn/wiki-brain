# Teddy

Teddy was a recurring Uniontown-area informal pharmaceutical supplier in
Dan's 2018–2020 message record. The relationship was transactional but not
anonymous: Teddy coordinated pickups around the country club, his father's
house, Fairchance, and other local landmarks; extended credit or held stock;
and periodically disclosed the fines, probation, family obligations, and
cash shortages that made his supply work precarious. He belongs in the
substance-use record as a local supply node, not as a close friend or a
reliable biographical narrator.

## Supply relationship

The messages begin in February 2018 and quickly settle into a repetitive
logistics pattern: Teddy asks what Dan needs, confirms amount and price,
coordinates a time or mailbox/vehicle handoff, and updates him on stock.
The vocabulary is abbreviated—"sbs," "strips," "adds," "bars," "zs," and
occasionally "blue" or "pink" tablets—so the record does not justify a
clean, item-by-item pharmacological inventory. It does show recurring access
to Suboxone strips and to pills described by color or brand, with quantities
and prices negotiated directly. Teddy also sometimes served as the contact
for a broader group, announcing what he had left and asking for advance
commitments before he bought more.

His operating geography overlaps Dan's caddying and Uniontown life. Messages
place him passing the country club, meeting near Arby's/Sunoco, working around
Fairchance, and timing exchanges around Dan's reports and work schedule. This
is a materially different role from a one-off dealer: over nearly two years,
Teddy repeatedly tried to reserve product, explain when upstream sources were
dry, and make handoffs despite his own limited transport and cash.

## Financial and legal precarity

Teddy's messages make clear that the trade was neither affluent nor stable.
In May and June 2018 he said he needed small outstanding amounts because of
fines, had borrowed money from his father to cover an order, and was trying to
complete an ARD/probation extension while paying it down. He described a
doctor's appointment, DUI class, community service, temporary work, car
problems, and the need to help on his father's roof. These statements are his
own account and should not be treated as independently verified legal history,
but they explain the constant emphasis on exact payment, advance notice, and
not leaving cash or product unattended.

The same instability appears in supply. At different points Teddy said a
source needed money that night, insurance might not cover a prescription,
someone had a hacked bank account, and a buyer had robbed him. In February
2019 he described beating a friend after saying that person had taken money
from him; this is an uncorroborated account in a transactional text thread,
not a fact established elsewhere. The important durable conclusion is more
modest: Teddy was operating within a fragile chain of personal debts and
short-notice acquisition, not a predictable retail system.

## Dan's place in the thread

Teddy treated Dan as a repeat customer he expected to pay and communicate
with, but also as someone worth accommodating. He held products, offered to
make a delivery before work or a class, and reassured Dan after Fran's death
that he could call for a ride or favor. The record also shows boundaries:
Teddy repeatedly asked whether Dan was certain before he committed money to
an order, and warned that he could not keep carrying uncertainty when another
buyer wanted the same stock.

In July 2019, Teddy vouched to an anxious third party whom Dan had invited to
New York, saying Dan would not strand him with a one-way ticket and that Dan
had the money to follow through. That is a narrow but useful social-graph
detail: Teddy knew Dan as someone who had left Uniontown and could sponsor a
trip, not only as a buyer. The final exchange, February 11, 2020, is Teddy
cleaning up his contacts and asking whether both Dan's and Annie's numbers
were still current. The corpus does not show why the thread then ends.

## Record limits

The master CSV marks its rows `Received` almost uniformly, but that field is
known to be unreliable; speaker attribution here is reconstructed from the
text and remains uncertain for messages that lack conversational context.
Teddy's full name, current status, sources, and the exact identity of every
abbreviated product are unknown. Nothing in this page should be read as a
diagnosis of his drug use, a complete criminal record, or proof of claims he
made about third parties.
