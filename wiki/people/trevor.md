# Trevor Bevins

Trevor Bevins was one of Dan's actual peers in the 2018 Nemacolin caddying
circle: a golf obsessive, drinking-and-gaming companion, and candid witness
to the dysfunction around [[wiki/people/brad-hubeaut|Brad Hubeaut]]'s yard.
The relationship was practical as well as social. They arranged reports,
rides, rounds at Uniontown Country Club and nearby courses, and later small
money transfers; Trevor's records also preserve Dan's uneven ability to repay
friends during the New York years. Their regular iMessage thread ends in
June 2020, but a Facebook exchange in February 2021 shows the connection
still alive through the meme-stock episode.

## The Nemacolin season

The dense part of the record is May–July 2018, when both men were working or
trying to work around the Nemacolin caddy program. Trevor offered rides,
planned post-report golf, and treated the job as an entry point into a larger
golf career rather than an identity in itself. He was serious about equipment
and instruction—arguing about shafts, launch, fitting, and club selection—and
his messages give the period much of its ordinary texture: rain forecasts,
whether enough loops were available, a spare white polo, a bucket at the
range, PUBG at night, beer, and attempts to fit in a round after work.

The thread also documents why the caddying arrangement felt untenable. Trevor
described days where fifty or sixty caddies were available for only a handful
of loops, tips were unreliable, and the program's economics made it rational
to look elsewhere. He thought Brad was burned out and resentful toward both
caddies and customers, though the friendship could still be useful for
accessing work and golf. In July, Trevor described a drunken Brad snapping a
club, abandoning him at a bar, agreeing to a $150 round and then disappearing
the next day. He returned to play with Brad once more, found him temporarily
calmer, and still concluded that the job was no longer viable.

## Leaving the yard

Trevor left in late July 2018 for Sand Valley, where he expected $110 per bag
and at least $220 for a 36-hole loop. His departure was not a rupture with
Dan: he thanked him directly for hanging out and playing golf during the
season, while explaining that early-check fees, low-paying reports, and being
passed over for an 8 a.m. report had made him "over it." That exchange makes
him a useful counterpoint to the more formal management record: the same
conditions that frustrated Dan were pushing a capable peer out of the yard.

By autumn Trevor was building a different working life. He talked about a
Club Champion fitting opportunity, launched or promoted a small caddy-gear
venture called Caddylands, and later said he was driving Uber and Lyft with a
new car. In September 2019 he was again looping and believed the job might
become full-time with benefits. The record does not establish a continuous
employment history, but it does show an active effort to convert golf knowledge
into steadier work rather than remain dependent on Nemacolin reports.

## Friendship, money, and later contact

Their friendship was easygoing and teasing, built around golf clips, sports
bets, music links, television, and the "#TrustYourCaddy" persona. Trevor knew
[[wiki/people/annie-ulmer|Annie]] well enough to ask whether a payment should
go to her Venmo, and he was in Dan's social orbit rather than merely a work
contact. At the same time, the late-2019 and May-2020 messages show a less
comfortable material layer. Dan apologized on Facebook after his phone shut
off and promised Trevor repayment plus extra for the trouble; Trevor was
patient in December 2019 but, in May 2020, asked not to be ignored and said
late repayment was a recurring issue. The record supports a friendship under
financial pressure, not an adversarial break.

During the first COVID months Trevor discussed reopening training, supply
problems, and friends working at Boeing. The final iMessage is June 18, 2020.
On February 4, 2021, their Facebook conversation turned to GameStop: Dan had
bought short-dated GME puts and called it irresponsible; Trevor said he had
closed his positions before the Reddit short squeeze and was sitting out a
market he considered distorted. That small exchange confirms continued rapport
and places Trevor at the edge of the [[wiki/timeline/periods/2020-2021-market-era|market era]],
without evidence that he was a major participant in it.

## Record limits

The master CSV's `direction` field is not reliable enough to assign every
iMessage speaker mechanically; the page identifies Trevor's role from the
content and uses the Facebook export, which names speakers, as the clearest
cross-check. The export does not establish where he lived after 2021, whether
Sand Valley became permanent, or whether the two remained in contact after
the meme-stock conversation.
