# Zachariah Harshman

Zachariah Harshman is a Laurel Highlands High School-era friend from
Uniontown whose decade-spanning Facebook Messenger thread (October
2011 – October 2021, roughly 970 messages) runs through three distinct
lives: a fellow burnout reminiscing about a drug-hazed senior year, a
2014–15 Uniontown weed and wax supplier operating out of the
[[wiki/places/155-virginia-ave|155 Virginia Avenue]] household, and —
after a genuine, ugly rupture — a platonic gaming friend who resurfaces
warmly in 2020 with nothing left of the earlier register at all. The
friendship's full arc, unusually, survives its own worst moment.

## High school, remembered

The earliest substantive exchange (October 2012) is pure shared
nostalgia for their senior year: "i seriously don't remember much of
2005/6," Dan writes, "i don't know if i've blocked it mentally or if it
was the amount of drugs," and Zach answers with pride rather than
concern — "Dude we killed it tho... N my senior year dude i did u
proud lol... We were hard as fuck tho we ran LH." It is one of the
corpus's few direct, contemporaneous confirmations that the addiction
arc predates the corpus proper and was locally legendary at the time,
not a private struggle — two friends toasting a shared reputation for
excess rather than processing it as harm.

## The SLOPPP pitch (January 2014)

Zach is [[wiki/interests/music/aliases/sloppp|SLOPPP]]'s earliest
documented booster. On January 28–29, 2014 — a year into the alias's
run — he spends a late night hyping a mix ("Dude the drop after the
roar samples was perfect... you a fool for this one"), then does
something no one else in the corpus is shown doing this early:
actively pitches Dan to the Pittsburgh rave scene. He connects Dan with
a contact named Timmie Sawyers ("she wants to watch u do your thing
before she contacts people") and pushes for a booking at a weekly rave
night at a Pittsburgh gallery venue. Dan sends over the SoundCloud link
and his email, describes his setup (a Numark NS7 II controller, Serato
DJ, production in Logic Pro X), and the two half-jokingly plan a studio
session — Zach wants beats to rap over, Dan promises "everything set
up here." Nothing documents whether the Timmie Sawyers booking
materialized, but the exchange is a rare primary window into SLOPPP
actively trying to break into a live scene, "desperate to play" a
first show, eighteen months before the alias closed.

## The supply relationship (2014–2015)

Beneath the music talk runs a second, denser thread: from mid-2014
through the end of 2015, Zach is a regular Uniontown supplier — wax,
dabs, "budder," and flower priced by the cut and quarter ("50/cut
90/q"), with occasional side asks in both directions (Zach floats a
one-time wax deal at double asking price from "my mom's connect";
Dan pays him for a ride). The 2014 handoffs reference "my mom" and give
no fixed address; from January 2015 onward the staging language turns
specific and consistent — "front door is open, i'm in the kitchen,"
"side door to garage," instructions to keep
[[wiki/people/alexis-armel|Alexis]] quiet while she's snuck upstairs —
matching the January 2015 move to
[[wiki/places/155-virginia-ave|155 Virginia Avenue]], the address the
[[wiki/mind/synthesis/bond-switch-2015|bond-switch synthesis]] later
identifies as the lair Annie inherits. This is a genuine gap closed in
the [[wiki/mind/synthesis/supply-network|supply network]] record: the
earliest previously-documented Uniontown nodes (Johnny, Teddy) date to
2018; Zach's thread pushes the network's attested history back three
years.

## The December 23, 2015 rupture

The relationship ends explosively, and precisely datably, three weeks
after [[wiki/people/annie-ulmer|Annie]] enters the picture. At 4:21 AM
on December 23, 2015, Dan sends a long, furious message accusing Zach
of "vanishing into thin air" and hearing about it "second hand" — Zach
had stopped bringing his supply business to the house. Zach's
explanation is the thread's real payload: he'd become
[[wiki/people/alexis-armel|Alexis]]'s "best friend other than you for
the past year," was spending regular time with her post-breakup, and
had pulled back specifically because of how it would look — "if you're
going to all of the sudden hang out 'all the time' with the ex
girlfriend of your best friend immediately after they break up, you
might want to make sure the aforementioned friend doesn't feel a
complete sense of betrayal." Dan does not deny any of it — "That's
your decision... Later dude and seriously. Good luck!" — and the
exchange closes with real hostility on both sides before trailing into
one long, conciliatory paragraph from Zach that goes unanswered for
nearly two months.

This is independent, third-party, real-time corroboration of exactly
what [[wiki/mind/synthesis/bond-switch-2015|the bond-switch
synthesis]] reconstructs from Dan's side of the corpus alone: the
Alexis-to-Annie transfer happened fast enough, and visibly enough, to
detonate a separate friendship in the same month. Zach is not
speculating from outside — he was inside Alexis's orbit at the exact
moment of the switch, and his anger is calibrated to having watched it
happen. The date also sits three days before
[[wiki/interests/music/aliases/sloppp|SLOPPP's]] own December 26, 2015
"Goodbye Demo" — a temporal adjacency worth noting, though nothing in
either thread asserts the two closures are connected.

## After the rupture: dormancy, then revival

The friendship does not stay dead, but it changes register permanently.
A brief, guarded check-in follows on February 18, 2016 ("Yo is
everything alright with Luke?" — Zach asking after
[[wiki/people/lukyan-mraz|Lukyan Mraz]], a shared friend named earlier
in the thread as an Xbox-modding console gamer). Contact through 2016–
2018 is sparse and largely transactional — a March 2017 "desperately
seeking oil" message is the last drug-adjacent ask in the record — then
goes fully dormant from late 2018 to November 2020.

When it resumes, in November 2020, the register has changed entirely:
two men in their thirties trading deep, technical, entirely sober
opinions about Skater XL and the Tony Hawk's Pro Skater remake — replay
value, trick mechanics, PS5 load times — with the warmth of the
original high-school friendship intact and none of its former
transactional content. The thread's last message (October 1, 2021) is
Zach circling back to a video Dan had sent him a week earlier, still
engaged. Whatever the 2015 rupture cost, it did not cost the
friendship permanently.
