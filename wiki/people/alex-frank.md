# Alex Frank

Dan's cousin — described by Dan as "semi-famous" — and, per public
sources, a real working journalist and editor based in Brooklyn covering
music, fashion, and culture. He was Deputy Editor at The FADER and Deputy
Culture Editor at Vogue.com, and has bylined at Vogue, The FADER,
Pitchfork, ELLE, Dazed & Confused, New York Magazine, Vulture, Fantastic
Man, the Village Voice, GQ, V Man, and The New York Times Style Magazine,
with celebrity profiles and interviews spanning Aretha Franklin, Bjork,
Tyler the Creator, Andre 3000, SZA, Young Thug, Kacey Musgraves, Virgil
Abloh, RuPaul, and many others.

Wrote a personal essay about [[wiki/people/morley-frank|Morley Frank]]'s
December 1998 funeral — the source of a detail Dan still references and
finds darkly funny: Eat'n Park (the Western PA restaurant chain) sent its
"Frownie" cookies (a frowning-face counterpart to the chain's famous
Smiley Cookie) to the gathering. An involuntary search-history pattern
(29 hits over the corpus's twenty-year span, with a 2025 cluster of 10
around "alex frank mac miller / gq / malcolm") shows Dan periodically
re-engaging with Alex's byline and career, consistent with ordinary
proud-cousin interest rather than anything more active in the
relationship.
