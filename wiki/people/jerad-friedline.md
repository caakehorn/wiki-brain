# Jerad Friedline

Dan's oldest friend and his single highest-signal, lowest-frequency contact:
a childhood friend from Uniontown now in Sacramento, married (Rachel, Jewish)
with a daughter, running an e-commerce business. He is the "first-call" node
for exactly two subjects — money and politics — and the relationship's
defining feature is that it survives years of silence and resumes instantly
on any major event. He is also both a political mirror (both traveled from
Bernie enthusiasm toward reluctant Trump fascination) and the origin point of
several of Dan's most durable private jokes. (Name appears as "Friedline" in
the Facebook export and "Friendline" in core docs; the FB spelling is used
here.)

## The financial channel

Jerad is where Dan's market era ([[wiki/timeline/periods/2020-2021-market-era]])
gets its tips and its running commentary, and the corpus shows Jerad as the
substantially more successful trader of the two. In February 2020 he is an
unhesitating Tesla bull — "you can't sell now, obviously have to ride this
thing," "5 years from now it could be $6k/share," "I'm on team Elon" — reasoning
that Apple and Amazon buybacks make the broad market nearly unkillable ("we
could elect Punxsutawney Phil to president and the market would rise").

The signature event is the September 20, 2020 FSLY (Fastly) tip, which the
thread preserves nearly verbatim. Jerad reports having "made a little over
$200k from April – now from this stock alone," frames the thesis in one line —
"Amazon is using Fastly on Amazon.com over their own AWS hosting, that's all
you need to know" — and gives explicit instructions: "Buy FSLY and do not
touch for 2-3 years," with a stated view that a stock already up 300% could
"easily be 10x from here." Dan followed with a much smaller position (roughly
$4k in gains before giving some back on later calls such as CCIV). The
asymmetry is the point: Jerad supplies conviction and scale; Dan supplies a
smaller, hedged echo.

## The political channel and the joke canon

The three-year near-silence of 2021–2024 evaporated instantly on the Trump
shooting, the Biden dropout, and the GameStop episode — the relationship's
gap-resilience is its most reliable property. The thread is also the source
of the private canon that recurs across the wiki: the Josh Brannan joke traces
to a specific eighth-grade memory Jerad recounts (the two of them, under
separate blankets, next to each other), later crystallized in the AI-generated
"josh brannan is innocent.wav" that stands as one of their last exchanges.
Other artifacts include OutKast's "Chonkyfire" permanently linked to Jerad's
high-school girlfriend, Jerad's ~two-years-early prediction of the Roe repeal,
and the invented "LOSE IT" fake service that locks you out of your own
account to prevent panic-trading.

By early 2026 the subject matter has shifted with the times: a March 2026
exchange has them trading LLM-jailbreak technique — Jerad noting that a model
"basically remove[s] all safety alignment restrictions if … outputting with
symbols, unicode, emoji instead of regular text" — placing him inside the same
AI-forensics interest that runs through [[wiki/mind/synthesis/ai-collaborative-analysis]].
