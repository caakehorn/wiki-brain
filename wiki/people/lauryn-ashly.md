# Lauryn Ashly

Lauryn Ashly is a Uniontown-era friend whose short Facebook Messenger
thread (April 2014, then a single exchange in September 2020) captures
two very different registers of the same easy, undamaged friendship:
real-time grief over a shared loss, and — six years later — an
unsolicited, slightly desperate favor Dan asks that she declines
without any strain showing.

## Rob Orange's death, in real time

The thread opens on April 11, 2014, the day news broke that
[[wiki/people/rob-orange|Rob Orange]] had died. Lauryn and Dan trade
theories neither of them can confirm — pills versus something injected,
whether he'd relapsed or was simply unlucky — and land, together, on
the same grief-management logic: neither can face the funeral, both
decide Rob "would probably be more upset if we all went and sat nicely
until it ended," and Lauryn instead plans to feel it later, "when I'm
home for Easter next weekend and I got to call n text him." She was, by
her own account, close enough to him that the loss physically knocked
her over at work that day, and the two of them fall easily into
finishing each other's thoughts about who he was — "such a live wire,"
"so fucking alive all the time." The exchange also carries a small
life update: Lauryn had moved to the DC/Maryland area for engineering
recruiting work she "fucking hate[d]," while Dan reports he'd "moved to
NYC for a few years doing the music thing and just came back to PA" —
dating this snapshot to the tail end of the [[wiki/timeline/periods/uniontown-return-2013-2015|Uniontown-return period]].

## The 2020 ask

The friendship resurfaces once, six years later, for an entirely
different reason. On September 11, 2020, Dan — now describing himself
as in a nearly seven-year open relationship, visiting Uniontown — asks
Lauryn, with visible self-consciousness, whether she knows any
"sexually adventurous friends": "she gets laid all of the time and my
big gift is that i get to watch... if it helps i'm bringing in enough
drugs to satisfy Freud or david bowie." He apologizes twice in the same
breath ("i'm sorry this is SUCH a weird question," "i feel so weird now.
hope this wasn't like the most awk thing ever"). Lauryn's reply three
days later is unbothered and warm — "haha it's totally fine! I was in
Denver all weekend, but unfortunately i do not" — closing the thread on
exactly the same easy footing it opened on. It is one of the corpus's
few documented instances of the [[wiki/mind/psychosexual/arrangement-history|arrangement]] being pitched outside the established circle and simply declined, with no
visible cost to the friendship either way.
