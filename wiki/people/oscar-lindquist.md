# Oscar Lindquist

Oscar Lindquist is a friend from Dan's Florida years whose entire
surviving footprint is a six-line Facebook Messenger thread (`724-208-
3475`, August–October 2011) — thin, but precisely dated, and the
primary source behind an anecdote otherwise preserved only as a
punchline on [[wiki/people/jason-bermejo|Jason Bermejo's page]].

## Hurricane Irene, dated

On August 26, 2011, Dan writes: "mohler and i are heading out of town
tonight because of the hurricane" — [[wiki/people/jamie-mohler|Jamie
Mohler]], evacuating together ahead of Hurricane Irene, which struck
the New York area two days later. Oscar's reply implies he'd made a
similar call already ("Haha, nice. I was going to go to Cocoa today
but I slept too long") and mentions plans to be "up there in a couple
of weeks." A follow-up on October 2, 2011 — "Shoot me a text/call if
you want to come down and meet up. I think today will be a decisive
day for the 99%ers" — places Oscar near the Occupy Wall Street action
that same autumn, the same protest movement documented in
[[wiki/people/jamie-mohler|Jamie's]] own 2011 chat log with Dan. Between
them, the two threads turn an undated "~autumn 2011" estimate into a
specific, corroborated week.

## The 2022 coincidence

The thread itself ends in 2011, but the friendship's best-known
moment happened a decade later and is preserved only secondhand, on
[[wiki/people/matt-dunn|Matt Dunn's page]]: on March 5, 2022, Dan was
waiting tables in midtown Manhattan when Oscar sat down in his section
— the "impossible coincidence in a 9M-person city" Jason's page
already carried as an unanchored anecdote. Nothing in Oscar's own
thread documents his side of that encounter or any contact
surrounding it; the reunion is known only through what Dan told Dunn
that night.
