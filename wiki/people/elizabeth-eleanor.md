# Elizabeth Eleanor

Elizabeth Eleanor is a fellow recovering addict whose entire
relationship with Dan is a single overnight Facebook exchange — July
10–11, 2013, just months after his return to
[[wiki/timeline/periods/uniontown-return-2013-2015|Uniontown]] from
NYC — that produced two of the corpus's more useful incidental data
points despite the two of them never speaking again on record.

## The conversation

Reconnecting after a long silence, the two quickly land on shared
history: both dealt with opiate addiction, and Elizabeth is several
years into 12-step recovery, crediting a "God" she's careful to define
loosely rather than through organized religion. She pushes Dan toward
NA/AA and the "Big Book"; he declines specifically and articulately,
citing his own psychological type — "i think my brain works a little
differently than most and as an INTP and heavily introverted,
logic-based dude...i can't see myself flourishing in AA/NA" — and
lays out, at length, a secular, self-reflective account of addiction
as "a neurological imbalance and a series of habitual behavior +
neglect...caused only by ourselves and fixed only by ourselves."
Elizabeth counters that the shared root problem is spiritual avoidance
("YOU are the problem, drugs and alcohol is just a symptom") and
recommends Donnie Darko as an emotional parallel to Dan's search. The
conversation is warm and genuinely searching on both sides, closing
with Elizabeth's sign-off: "I respect your views... I hope you can
prove me wrong though."

## What it incidentally documents

Buried in the recovery debate are two facts that don't appear together
anywhere else in the corpus. First, a precise **maintenance dose**: "i'm
still on 2mg of suboxone a day, it's been almost 5 years since i
touched an opiate and i'm still too terrified to get off" — the only
concrete dosage figure for the [[wiki/health/chemical-architecture|
fifteen-year Suboxone regimen]] on record anywhere, and one that backs
into a last-opiate-use date of roughly 2008–2009, independently
consistent with the corpus's [[wiki/timeline/periods/dark-era-2007-2008|
dark-era]] window. Second, the earliest dated instance of Dan
explicitly self-typing **INTP** — three years before the personality
material that dominates [[wiki/mind/profile/intp|the later profile
pages]] — used here as a specific, reasoned explanation for rejecting
group-based recovery rather than as abstract self-description.
