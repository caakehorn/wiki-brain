# Music as Identity

The thesis: for Dan, music is not a hobby or a "top artists" list. It is
one of the few stable structures of self that has run the entire length of
the documented life — as a teenage identity performed in public (the 2007
Facebook layer), as a literal itinerary (the tour-following years), as a
private emotional regulator (the Elliott Smith pole), and as a professional
identity (the producer-alias lineage). The wiki's old habit of filing each
artist as a one-line favorites entry systematically under-reported this,
because the meaning was in the *through-line*, not the tracks.

## The four modes music has actually served

1. **Public self, performed.** The 2007 Facebook statuses — "swooning over
   the new FOB," "Say Anything obsession," "slaying on halo," "Super Mario
   Galaxy" — are a self presented to the world as a coordinated aesthetic
   stance. Music (and gaming) was part of the mask, not behind it.

2. **Itinerary / devotion.** He toured the mid-2000s scene: three Fall Out
   Boy shows in eleven months plus a release-day TRL taping, a Say Anything
   date in Baltimore, Bamboozle at the Meadowlands
   ([[wiki/timeline/events/teen-concert-years]]). This is fandom as
   logistics. The secret-show alias "Schrute Farms"
   ([[wiki/interests/the-office]]) shows he was let in on the in-joke, not
   watching from outside it.

3. **Emotional regulator.** Elliott Smith is the #1 individual artist in a
   library otherwise built on bass-heavy electronic warfare — the
   "quietest, most intimate presence" that proves the emotional range
   extends to its opposite pole
   ([[wiki/interests/favorites/music/artists/elliott-smith]]). The 2025
   "R&B before 1979 or it's trash… go back to elliott smith" gate shows
   the regulation is still live and still enforced.

4. **Professional identity.** Since ~2013 he has been a producer under
   four aliases (SLOPPP → MOGZART → MOGGED-UP → GRIPNOTIC), all
   sub-bass-dominant, all unified by an involuntary low-frequency signature
   ([[wiki/interests/music/overview]]). The move to electronic was, per the
   profile, a correct read of where authentic expression lived after rock's
   1963 chart-peak — not a retreat. Production is the architecture; he is a
   producer-first identity, not a performer.

## The cutoff as a personality signature

The affection has edges that reveal the mechanism. Fall Out Boy love stops
at *Folie à Deux* (2008) and does not extend to the post-hiatus reunion
band ([[wiki/interests/favorites/music/artists/fall-out-boy]]). Say
Anything and the emo cluster are claimed up to the 2010 imprinting window
and then frozen. The pattern — intense, whole-catalog engagement with a
thing, followed by a hard refusal to follow it past a self-set boundary —
mirrors the relationship architecture documented elsewhere: maximum
absorption, then a clean cutoff, with the pre-cut version held as the "real"
one. Music preference is where this shows up cleanest, because the objects
can't betray him.

## Why this is its own page

The "unutilized for its actual purpose" directive that prompted this pass
is, at root, about music: the favorites entries were being used as a
catalog when they are actually a self-portrait. This page is the spine that
connects the artist pages, the concert-years event, the Office in-joke, and
the production overview into one claim — that music is a primary,
century-spanning structure of self, not a list. Each linked page carries
one facet; this one states the whole.
