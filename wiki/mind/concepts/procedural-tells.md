# Procedural Tells

**Definition:** Observable deviations from expected institutional, professional, or social protocols that serve as high-signal evidence of underlying dynamics (error, cover, directed action, or power play). These are prioritized over subjective impressions because they are falsifiable and documented.

## Core Principle
Absence of expected procedure (paper trail, perimeter, chain of custody, documentation) is frequently the load-bearing signal rather than the presence of overt action. "The absence of paper trail is the feature."

## Key Examples from Sources

**Drawer Shortage (BFS):**
- CSR closing report at close: "nothing over or under."
- Next-day AM claim: "$50 short... I paid it out of my own pocket, you owe me."
- Tell: Personal extraction with zero employer record. Bypasses PA wage deduction rules and standard accounting escalation.
- Implication: Either AM covering own error/theft or running off-books skim; assigning to path of least resistance.

**J6 Capitol Events (raw footage analysis):**
- No perimeter established after lethal force in volatile environment.
- "Medical" response limited to cosmetic actions (unzipping jacket) with no CPR or airway management.
- Door blocker positioned at key access point exhibiting non-chalant, directed behavior (requires knowledge of layout and purpose).
- Proud Boy window-smasher cuffed then released.
- Eugene Goodman herding sequence ignores obvious high-value open door (Senate chambers/Romney evac).
- Tell: Multiple textbook violations clustered in minutes. "Execution didn't need to be clean if the product is imagery."

**Manipulation / Relationship Data (master prompt + prior corpora):**
- Sustained cause/effect inversion and semantic drift in communication.
- Insight deployed by one party with no behavioral change in the other.
- "Forensic over-explanation" as a pattern that serves proof-of-rightness more than resolution.

## Detection Method
1. Establish baseline expected procedure (from training, policy, historical precedent, or common practice).
2. Identify mismatch with observed record (report, video, message log, witness statement).
3. Test alternative explanations (chaos, incompetence, shock) against the specific procedural gap.
4. If gap requires coordination, knowledge, or motive to explain, elevate as tell.
