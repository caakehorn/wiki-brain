# Taboo and Boundary-Testing

The dossier material frames Dan's interest in sexual and social taboo not
as transgression-seeking for its own charge, but as an application of the
same cognitive habit documented across the rest of the typology cluster:
find the edge condition of a system, then push past it to see whether the
category structure holds. `Dan Profile.txt` names this "taboo as ontology
rupture" — "fracturing the very categories that define identity" — and
gives one concrete named example: a stated fascination with orchestrating
sexual contact that crosses stated orientation ("orientation violation"),
watching for what the report calls the collapse of a stable category
rather than pursuing the encounter for conventional erotic charge.

## What this connects to elsewhere in the profile

This is presented in the source material as a direct extension of the
Ti-Ne cognitive pattern already established on [[wiki/mind/profile/intp]]
and [[wiki/mind/profile/enneagram-5w4]]: "compulsive curiosity to find edge
conditions and bypass arbitrary limits," applied here to sexual and
relational categories instead of arguments, institutions, or belief
systems. The deviance audit's independent 96/100 score for the
"psychosexual operating system" ([[wiki/mind/profile/deviance-mapping]])
is consistent with this framing — it explicitly describes the underlying
behavior as *engineered*, not impulsive, which matches the general finding
across that whole audit that Dan's documented deviance clusters around
deliberate construction rather than reactive behavior.

## The evidentiary gap

Unlike [[wiki/mind/psychosexual/orchestration-and-voyeurism]], which has a
richly corroborated practice layer (the arrangement history), this
specific mechanism — taboo-seeking as category-rupture rather than
transgression-for-its-own-sake — has **no independent primary-source
example** in the corpus searched for this page. No message thread, tweet,
or first-person account names an "orientation violation" encounter, a
specific instance of it, or Dan's own explanation of what drew him to it.
The claim exists only inside the single AI-authored dossier, stated once,
without elaboration or corroborating detail (no date, no named parties,
no outcome). It should be read as the thinnest-supported claim in this
entire cluster — plausible given everything else documented about the
cognitive style, but resting on a single unelaborated sentence rather than
a documented event.

## What the documented arrangement history does support

The broader claim — that Dan treats social/sexual boundaries as something
to be tested and pushed rather than accepted as given — has better,
independent support from the arrangement history itself, even without a
specific orientation-violation example. The pattern of orchestrating
encounters across every category the arrangement touches — his own
ex-partner ([[wiki/people/alexis-armel|Alexis]]), a paid professional
relationship that blurred into friendship
([[wiki/people/kelly-johansson|Kelly Johansson]]), a woman he was also
dating one-on-one at the same time
([[wiki/people/shelbie-breakiron|Shelbie Breakiron]]) — reads as a
consistent refusal to keep the arrangement inside a single stable
category (friend, stranger, professional, ex), which is the same
boundary-blurring instinct the "ontology rupture" framing describes in
the abstract. See [[wiki/mind/psychosexual/arrangement-history]] for the
full inventory.

**Gaps:** the orientation-violation claim specifically remains
unsourced beyond a single sentence in one AI-authored report. If a primary
source for this ever surfaces, it should replace this page's framing
rather than supplement it, since there is currently nothing concrete to
supplement.
